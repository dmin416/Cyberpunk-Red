## Housing Changes

*Source: InfiniteFlames*

In addition to the usual housing effects, these apply based on your living conditions. Downtime lost from lack of housing is automatically spent doing a downtime active simply called **Surviving**.

### Living on the Streets

- **-2 DTD per month** (this DTD is automatically spent Surviving).
- **DV15** resist torture or drugs or lose **2d6 Humanity**.
- You are subject to the elements and weather.

### Living on the Streets in a Vehicle

- **-2 DTD per month** unless you have a housing upgrade (this DTD is automatically spent Surviving).

### Living in the Wilderness

- **DV15 Wilderness Survival** check; if failed, lose **2d6 Humanity** and you are subject to the weather.

### Cube Hotel

No change.

### Cargo Container

No change.

### Studio Apartment

No change.

### Two-Bedroom Apartment / Corporate Conapt

You count as having the **Moral Boost** HQ Improvement. If you upgrade this building to a HQ, you start with this improvement for free.

### Upscale Conapt / Beaverville House

You count as having the **Moral Boost** HQ Improvement, as well as one other of your choice. If you upgrade this building to a HQ, you start with these improvements for free.

### Luxury Penthouse / Beaverville McMansion

You count as having the **Moral Boost** HQ Improvement as well as one other of your choice. If you upgrade this building to a HQ, you start with these improvements for free. In addition, these HQs start with the **Upkeep Reduction** Improvement for free.

## Headquarters

*Source: InfiniteFlames*

Sure choomba, you might have a place to stay, but do you have a headquarters? Housing can be upgraded to headquarters, but it costs extra to maintain. To be a valid HQ, your housing must have room for at least two bedrooms, though two neighbouring cargo containers may be renovated to connect and count as a single building for the purposes of the HQ.

When you upgrade your HQ, you get benefits based on how much you pay for upkeep, separated into 4 tiers. The number of features you may have is decided by your tier, but you may never have more HQ features than the amount of bedrooms your HQ has room for. Upgrades do not count against this limit.

### Tier 1

- Up to **4** Headquarter Features.
- **Cost:** 500eb/month

### Tier 2

- Up to **6** Headquarter Features.
- **1** free Headquarter Feature of your choice.
- **Cost:** 1500eb/month

### Tier 3

- Up to **8** Headquarter Features.
- Grants **1** additional bedroom to your HQ.
- **2** free Headquarter Features of your choice.
- **Cost:** 3000eb/month

### Tier 4

- Up to **10** Headquarter Features.
- Grants **2** additional bedrooms to your HQ.
- **3** free Headquarter Features of your choice.
- **Cost:** 5000eb/month

### HQ IP Gain

Every month you pay for upkeep, you get **40 HQ IP**. If you move, you can refund IP as long as your base was not destroyed. Moving HQ costs **500eb**.

### Changes to HQ Features

**Workstation:** No eb gain for teammates. Teammates still follow loyalty rules; upgrade instead [insert upgrade idea here]

**Rent Reduction:** Replaced with **Upkeep Reduction**.

**Upkeep Reduction:** Reduce the monthly upkeep of your HQ by one category. Will not affect the upkeep of the month it is obtained. A Tier 1 HQ only costs **100eb** in upkeep with this. Can be upgraded for extra rooms as per Rent Reduction. Does not count against HQ feature limit.

## Token System

*Source: InfiniteFlames*

"Tokens" are an effective way of reimbursing characters for death and loss, and provide effects lasting past a character's death.

Every time a character goes on a gig, they earn a **Retirement Token**, which should be kept track of at all times.

Retirement Tokens remain with a character and cannot be used until they die or retire.

Each Retirement Token is worth **500eb** and **50 IP**, regardless of the payout of the gig.

When a character retires, they halve their number of Retirement Tokens and can then use their new eb and IP on the next character they make. You cannot reserve any amount of Retirement Tokens; they must all be used on the next character, or be forfeited.

If the character dies in a way that is determined **glorious** (meaning not from suicide, while under their own control, for a cause or to protect someone, etc.), they receive their entire retirement reward instead. If the GM cannot decide whether it counts as glorious, **25%** of Retirement Tokens can be removed instead.

For each **5 Tokens** acquired, their next character starts with a **+1 generic REP**.

Should a character die a glorious death with **20 or more tokens**, they are celebrated with a special memorial forum, and they receive their own drink at the Afterlife.

## Stop the Bleed!

*Source: Red Skies Homebrew Suggestion*

### Introduction

#### What is this?

My goal with this suggestion is to implement bleeding in Cyberpunk RED in a way that leads to increased tactical flexibility and decision making. In combat, a bleed can force you to consider your options and is a dilemma that must be addressed. This supplement is designed to, at its core, add that tactical complexity without bogging down the game, or creating unfun situations.

#### How do I start bleeding?

When you take a Critical Injury, you start to bleed. This is a **Light Bleed** unless you take a serious Critical Injury:

- **Body Critical Injuries** that cause a **Heavy Bleed:** Dismembered Arm, Dismembered Hand, Crushed Fingers, Dismembered Leg
- **Head Critical Injuries** that cause a **Heavy Bleed:** Lost Eye, Lost Ear

**Expansive Ammo** (and similar) are unique in that any bleed they cause will automatically be a **Heavy Bleed**.

#### Ouch! What does that mean?

Bleeding is a passive, inconsistent, and terrifying source of damage that is surprisingly easy to treat. The core objective of these rules is to add that kind of fear that is induced when you, in real life, experience or witness serious bleeding. It should be frantic, scary, but ultimately something that can be solved with First Aid skills.

**Light Bleed**

- Causes the victim to take **1d6/2 (round up)** Damage direct to HP at the end of each of their turns.
- Can be **paused** by spending either your Move or your Action to put pressure on the wound and staunch the bleeding for the rest of the turn.
- Can only be **stopped permanently** by spending an Action to make a **DV13** First Aid or Paramedic check to stop the bleeding.

**Heavy Bleed**

- Causes the victim to take **1d6** Damage direct to HP at the end of each of their turns.
- Can be **paused** by spending either your Move or your Action to put pressure on the wound and staunch the bleeding.
- Can only be **stopped permanently** outside of combat by spending **1 Minute** to make a **DV15** First Aid or Paramedic check to stop the bleeding.

**Bleeds do not stack.** If you are suffering from a Light Bleed and take another critical injury, you do not start bleeding twice. When you take a Heavy Bleed, it overrides your previous Light Bleed, and you are now only considered to be suffering from a Heavy Bleed.

Stabilizing the **Mortally Wounded** state also staunches all bleeds. The bleeding resumes when the character resumes consciousness.

#### I'm Still Bleeding Out!

Bleeding can and **will** kill an unconscious character who is not given medical attention, but a conscious character who survived a fight will almost certainly pull through. Outside of combat, your character may passively maintain pressure on the wound as they head to their friendly Ripperdoc for treatment, or to Trauma Team, where it can be fixed for **100eb**. Fixing these sorts of bleeds does not cost any DTDs. Stabilizing from the mortally wounded state cures all bleeds.

## Ablaze

*Source: Blue*

This ruling is not optional for things that require it for their normal functions (like the Napalmer).

Ablaze is a new way to set things on fire — specifically surfaces.

It has **3 tiers:** Mild, Strong, and Deadly.

### Ablaze (Mild / Strong / Deadly)

Any target passing through an area that has been set ablaze must make a **DV (11 / 13 / 15) Evasion** check or take **(1d6 / 2d6 / 3d6)** ablaze damage and be set **On Fire (Mild / Strong / Deadly)**. Whether or not this sets the target on fire without dealing damage through armor depends on the source of the Ablaze.

- This can only occur **once per round per target**.
- Cannot deal critical injuries.

Ablaze is dealt at the **start of your turn** whenever you enter an affected area in that round.

The duration of ablaze depends on what is stated in the item description, or what the GM decides.

Additionally, ablaze damage is treated as **fire damage that does not ignore armor**.

## Upgrading Installed Cyberware

*Source: Blue*

Look, doing this in RAW just kind of completely sucks in every way imaginable. So, we decided to do something about that.

Upgrading installed cyberware now works like this:

1. You get a **Tech** to make the upgrade as an item.
2. A **Medtech** can install it with a surgery check at a **DV equal to whatever the install DV was** for the cyberware being upgraded.

### Example

*Kat (Tech) and an unnamed Medtech upgrading an Internal Linear Frame Sigma with Compact Framing:*

1. **Kat** makes a **Compact Framing** upgrade item by spending **14 DTD** and passing a **DV24** upgrade check (or invention check, since she invented the upgrade) for her Internal Linear Frame Sigma.
2. **Kat** contacts a Medtech to install the Compact Framing upgrade into her Internal Linear Frame Sigma.
3. The **Medtech** performs a **DV17** surgery check (since the Internal Linear Frame Sigma requires hospital installation), spends **4 hours of DTD**, and consumes the Compact Framing upgrade item to install it onto Kat's Internal Linear Frame Sigma.

If the Medtech **fails** the check, they lose the downtime and have to try again. The Compact Framing upgrade is **not lost** in this event.

## Luck Rework

*Source: InfiniteFlames*

The following changes are made to how LUCK works on the server.

- **LUCK** can be spent **after** knowing the outcome of a roll, rather than exclusively before the roll is made.
- **LUCK** refills at the **start of a gig**, the **end of a gig**, and after **weekly XP levels are reset**.
- Although you can technically refill LUCK during a gig, you **cannot spend** any of that refilled LUCK in the gig — only the LUCK refilled at the **start of a gig** can be used.
- At any time, a GM can declare a **session break**, which refills LUCK. This refilled LUCK **can** be used in a gig.
- LUCK spent **after** a roll adjusts the result by **+1 per point**.
- LUCK spent **before** the roll adjusts by **+2 per point**, but **during a gig only**.

## Cyberpsychosis Stage 5 Rework

*Source: Blue*

So, negative humanity is a thing, and in RAW, you just give the GM full control of your character and they just kinda... die.

It's way more fun in RAW though since there's still an actual interaction that occurs and your GM actually fully knows how you play your character. Unfortunately, since players in this server jump from GM to GM constantly, that's really not something that can reasonably be done to a satisfying degree. Also GMs don't have your Tuppers.

So! A slight rework is in order!

Basically, we're going to treat it somewhat similarly to an Exec teammate's loyalty checks.

When a character is a **Stage 5 cyberpsycho**, a GM can roll a **negative d6**. If the result is **lower** than the character's would-be¹ negative empathy, the player keeps control (e.g., the GM rolls **-2**, the player would have **-1** empathy — the player maintains control).

This can be repeated **once per turn in combat**, or whenever the GM feels it's appropriate outside of combat.

**Note:** PVP is always enabled for cyberpsychos. (Don't be an asshole though.) Technically, GM control does not count as PVP.

### For GMs

- Don't spam it outside of combat.
- In combat, the GM may only take control for the **one turn** where the roll was failed. One failed roll does **not** carry over into the next turn.
- Out of combat, you may give a task (or tell the player to sabotage a task) instead of taking over for a turn, just like a loyalty check.
- Be realistic. These characters are insane, not stupid. A controlled character should still have some level of reason, at least from their point of view. A cowardly cyberpsycho is far more likely to run away or freeze up in the heat of combat rather than go on a sudden rampage. Hallucinations are a universal thing for Stage 5 cyberpsychos, so do with that what you will.
- Ultimately, just make an attempt to direct the player into playing their character to their worst tendencies like you would in RAW, except the player is still technically the one in control.
- If a player is already playing their character to their absolute worst tendencies, you don't have to make them roll the cyberpsychosis check.

### Negative Humanity → Negative Empathy

| Humanity | Would-Be Empathy |
|----------|------------------|
| -1 to -10 | -1 |
| -11 to -20 | -2 |
| -21 to -30 | -3 |
| -31 to -40 | -4 |
| -41 to -50 | -5 |
| -51 or lower | You've completely lost it. There's no coming back. |

¹ **"Would be"** because the lowest any of your stats can go is 0. Only in this specific case is empathy able to be treated as below 0.

### Cyberpsychosis Stages (Reference)

| Stage | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| **Stage 1** | Not a cyberpsycho. **>2 EMP** |
| **Stage 2** | Borderline Cyberpsychopathy. **2 EMP** |
| **Stage 3** | **3** Hare traits. **1 EMP** |
| **Stage 4** | **5** Hare traits. **0 EMP** |
| **Stage 5-1** | Negative humanity. **-1 EMP** |
| **Stage 5-2** | Negative humanity. **-2 EMP** |
| **Stage 5-3** | Negative humanity. **-3 EMP** |
| **Stage 5-4** | Negative humanity. **-4 EMP** |
| **Stage 5-5** | Negative humanity. **-5 EMP** |
| **Stage 6** | **-6 EMP** or less. You cannot come back from this — no matter what, you cannot regain humanity. |

## Role Changes

*Source: InfiniteFlames*

### Solo

- **Damage Deflection:** ALL damage reduced by X (was first damage taken).
- **Spot Weakness:** All damage dealt increased by X (was first damage dealt).
- **Improve Perception** and **Initiative** merged into a single skill: **Vigilance**.
- **Precision Attack** returns itself at a **2-for-1** ratio (was 3-for-1).
- **Fumble Recovery** has a second tier of functionality when all **10** Solo points are poured into it, granting **nat 1 immunity on Evasion rolls** also.

### Exec

- Exec **Team Members** get IP equal to **half** the amount the Exec receives, which can only be spent on **skills**. The HQ feature gives them **full IP**. Teammates still follow loyalty rules.

### Nomad

- Nomads may **redistribute their Moto ranks** each time they rank up, as long as they trade in their rigs as normal.
- The **housing capacity** upgrade may be taken **twice**. A groundcar with two housing upgrades is considered to be an **RV with two bedrooms**.

### Fixer

**Sourcing by Rank**

- Fixers starting at **Rank 3** can always source **Expensive** items.
- Fixers **Rank 7** and above can always source **Very Expensive** items.
- Fixers of **Rank 9** can source **Luxury** items.
- Fixers of **Rank 10** can source **any item in the game**, no questions asked.

**DTD & Sourcing Rules**

- Sourcing gear for someone else is **1 DTD per item**.
- Only **one unique type of item** can be sourced per DTD, but in any quantity you want. E.g., 10 Stolbovoy Assault Rifles could take **1 DTD**, but 4 Stolbovoys and a Sternmeyer Shotgun would take **2 DTDs**.
- Applying a **Haggle** to a service or item, even for personal use, is **1 DTD** but can be done in the **same DTD** as sourcing an item, for free.
- **Operator** sourcing DVs now match the **Tech DVs** for the price categories.
- Any item brought to a **Night Market** is sourced for free, but would need a DTD if you plan on haggling.

**What does this mean to you, the friendly Fixer?**

Instead of giving people items for what it cost you to source them, you may want to charge a little higher. When sourcing a pair of Smart Gun Links, for instance, charge **1000eb** and pocket the **10%** discount. Take advantage of **Buy 5 Get 1** to make sure you can profit off of these sorts of transactions. Net on net, this will, at worst, increase the amount your customers are paying by about **10%**. They will not resent you for this. Now, you are more in line with Techs and Medtechs who spend significant time to do the labor they do, usually profiting less per month than you even after these balancing changes. No Woman. No Cry.

**Example Transaction**

Bones comes to their friendly Fixer and asks for:

- 1 Militech Ronin Hyperlight Assault Rifle
- 3 Smartgun Links
- 6× Pharma Materials

This costs **600eb**, **1500eb**, and **1200eb**.

The Fixer makes their haggles, and passes them! They spend **3 DTDs**, pay their sources **540eb**, **1350eb**, and **1080eb**.

Now it's up to them to charge Bones a fair price for 3 days of making phone calls and inspecting shipments. The Fixer quotes a cool **3200eb**, and Bones happily pays it. Everyone lives happily ever after.

### Tech

**Inventions**

- Inventions, in addition to inventing the item, now cost **materials**. The cost is the same as fabrication.
- In exchange, you gain a **copy of the item** upon inventing it.
- Techs can choose to **fabricate or upgrade** with an **invention check** instead, as long as they were the one who invented the item or upgrade.

**Patents**

- All patents are **open**. Any Tech can make any invention freely. However, **Fixers cannot source Tech inventions**.

**Repairs**

- No change for vehicles.

**Repairing Armor**

| Damage | SP Remaining | DTD Cost |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Mild | More than half SP | 3 hours |
| Major | Half or less SP | 1 day |
| Destroyed | Zero SP | 3 days |

This also applies to **shields**.

Non-armor repair time is **fabrication DTD -1 tier**.

### Medtech

Therapy sucked. So we adjusted the values for Medtechs and added a new cost category.

| Therapy | DTD | DV | Cost | Humanity Restored | Hospital Cost |
|---------|-----|-----|------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Standard** | 7 | 15 | 100eb | 2d6 | 500eb |
| **Extreme** | 7 | 17 | 250eb | 4d6 | 1,000eb |
| **Ultra** | 7 | 21 | 1,000eb | 8d6 | 5,000eb |

## Misc Changes

Bodysculpting now gives you a **+1 to COOL**. Live your best life and look fine doing it.

*Source: Blue*

Techs and Medtechs do **not** need to source their materials from Fixers and can always acquire them at **base material cost**.

### Multiclass Hustle

*Source: InfiniteFlames*

The core rulebook says that if you start taking ranks in a new Role, you're treated as an up-and-coming person of that Role, and are no longer seen as your old Role. That's dumb. **Ignore that rule.**

When hustling, however, you must **declare what Role you are hustling as** before you roll.

### Exotic Bodysculpting

*Source: InfiniteFlames*

Exotic bodysculpting at **character creation** does **not** incur humanity loss. All other times you must still roll for humanity loss.

## Cyberware Changes

*Source: InfiniteFlames*

### Tech Hair

This new updated hardware for Tech Hair allows it to change its length through nanite extension from its natural length (down to a buzz cut) to a full **8m** length. For more elaborate designs and simple styling, it can also grow out unevenly as the user desires.

This change will be added to Tech Hair's current abilities — **no upgrade or payment needed**. It just allows easier character flavoring and more style options so you're not stuck with your default hairstyle.

As there is no deep functional change, changing your hair length and style does **not** take an action unless it requires styling like elaborate or complex styles.
