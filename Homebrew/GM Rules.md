# GM Rules [[WIP]]

*Source: Blue*

Guidance and house rulings for Game Masters.

---

## All GMs

- All GMs can only host **one gig at a time**. This includes easy gigs.
- All gigs must be posted in **gig-ic** and **gig-ooc** respectively.

### GM Payout (Per Hosted Gig)

One of your characters receives the following when hosting:

| Difficulty | eb | IP |
|------------|-----|-----|
| **Easy** | 250 | 20 |
| **Mild** | 750 | 70 |
| **Hazardous** | 2500 | 250 |
| **Lethal** | 4000 | 400 |
| **Hyper Lethal** | Whatever amount is approved |

If the players lose, you still gain that amount.

### General Guidelines

- **Lethal gigs should be lethal.** Make sure the gig's payment matches its difficulty.
- **Do not use GMPCs** in any hosted gigs. You can have NPCs that follow the players, but they shouldn't be in the spotlight.
- **Recurring NPCs are allowed.** (For example, having multiple gigs hosted by the same Fixer is fine.)
- Make sure the **listed difficulty** for your gigs is accurate. Do not allow unprepared characters into difficult gigs. If players are going to struggle, it shouldn't be because their teammates are dragging them down.
- If a player character **intentionally sabotages** a gig, that counts as **PVP** and must be agreed upon by all involved players out of character before it happens.
- Some gigs may be **shadowed by staff**. For trial GMs, this may be a requirement depending on multiple factors.
- All **event gigs** must be approved by the **Story Director** before they can be listed. There will probably only be one event gig occurring at a time, though this might not always be the case.
- **Don't railroad.** Having consequences for player actions is not railroading, but preventing them from making their own decisions is. Make sure consequences are reasonable.
- Let staff know if a player becomes an issue.
- Make sure you tag your gigs **"Complete"** once they end (the forum post).

### Loot Limits

Gigs have a **loot value limit**. The value of loot obtained cannot exceed the difficulty threshold. For example: a **Hazardous** gig cannot offer more than **3,000eb** worth of loot per player, since that is the maximum payout for a Hazardous gig in eb.

Any loot exceeding this value should be treated as **unsellable** at the end of the gig and useless (incompatible, irreparably broken, etc.). With GM permission, players may choose which loot they keep as long as it doesn't exceed their available loot budget. **This is a hard limit.** Gigs with higher payouts for their difficulty rating should steer away from reaching this maximum limit.

**Gig loot must become a RAW (or server-approved) item** if it isn't at the end of the gig. You can't give a player a homebrew item and have it work outside of the gig.

**Example:** You give a player a satchel charge to blow open a wall, but they decide to hold onto it instead of using it. That satchel charge becomes a **grenade** (mechanically) when the gig ends. Alternatively, you can just make the item junk.

## Gig Difficulty Tiers (Street Level)

### Easy

**0–500eb | 0–50 IP | 1 rep max**

A cake walk for anyone. Stupidity is the only potentially lethal thing here.

**Example:** A team of combat-incapable characters go to a party and win some harmless bets.

---

### Mild

**500–1,500eb | 50–150 IP | 3 rep max**

A team of beginners could struggle, but the chance of any of them dying or failing is very low.

**Examples:**

- **Low end:** A store clerk decided to spread lies about someone. Your goal is just to scare him.
- **High end:** A couple gangers and their boss have illegally occupied an abandoned building recently purchased by a smaller corp. The corp wants 'em gone. Goal is to clear them out without levelling the building. NCPD tend to avoid the area but will arrive after a while if you make too much noise.

---

### Hazardous

**1,500–3,000eb | 150–300 IP | 5 rep max**

A team of experienced edgerunners could struggle. High chance of injury and a low chance of death. Beginners could win, but it's a gamble at best.

**Example:** A cyberpsycho has apparently taken over a building and C-SWAT is stretched too thin to deal with it. Goal is to kill him. He's been there a while and is obsessed with building traps. There's a survivor in the building you need to rescue.

---

### Lethal

**3,000–5,000eb | 300–500 IP | 7 rep max | Requires 4 rep to enter**

If your team isn't prepared, expect death. Even veterans would struggle to win. Victory can still be achieved.

**Example:** Storm and hold a ganger compound that knows a powerful force is coming and is ready for a fight. The compound is in their territory, so backup will arrive. One of them has a rocket launcher. The place is trapped and heavily fortified. You can't just blow it all up either.

---

### Hyper Lethal

**5,000–7,500eb | 500–750 IP | 10 rep max**

Everything listed on this line is just a suggestion. Even Adam Smasher would think twice. Veterans would struggle significantly. High chance of death; even winning is a gamble. A suicide mission for any beginner.

**Example:** Storming a ganger compound? Nah — you're gonna storm a **megacorp HQ**. When you do, they'll send everything they have. Tanks, large drones, whatever the fuck that thing is in Cynosure — it's all fair game. Expect to die. That can all happen before you complete your first objective. If you win, you'll be a legend.

## Penalty Gigs

Penalty gigs are **forced gigs** that players can receive as punishments for certain actions (e.g., having negative EMP).

- Penalty gigs should **not** give any eb, IP, or rep rewards to the player. Minor loot is fine.
- The goal isn't to get paid — it's to **survive**.
- Difficulty should generally scale with the involved player's **total money spent** (up to **200,000eb** max), **total IP spent** (up to **4,000** max), and **total rep**. This is visible in the archives section of the player's character sheet.
- Gamemasters may run penalty gigs with **staff approval** (some may already be pre-approved).

---

## Gamemaster Role

- Gamemasters can run **Mild**, **Hazardous**, or **Lethal** gigs freely. Going beyond that difficulty and payment requires **staff approval**.
- Gamemasters may run **penalty gigs** with staff approval.
- **Player limit:** 6 players per gig (subject to change if it becomes an issue).

## Mercenary Level Gigs

Interface RED Volume 5 is in use. **Read the guidelines in Interface RED Volume 5.**

Mercenary level gigs are harder than street level. That much is obvious.

- **Trial GMs** cannot run any Mercenary level gig.
- **Gamemasters** must first gain **approval** to run Mercenary gigs.
- **Do not balance** Mercenary gigs based on player count or player combat ability. Per Interface 5: *"A military base isn't going to magically have less people just because only one person is attacking it."*
- If new players apply and **none of them die** on a Mercenary Level Lethal, you **severely underbalanced** your gig.

> **"My players don't want their characters to die and are upset that they're about to. What do I do?"**
>
> Kill them anyway. When you sign up for Hazardous or higher Mercenary Level gigs, you know what can happen. If they didn't want to die, they shouldn't have signed up.
