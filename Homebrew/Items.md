## Hello Cutie! — Love at First Shot

Hello, cutie! Feeling lonely?

Let's be real for a sec, choom. Night City's a cold place. A screaming vortex of concrete canyons, flickering neon, and a million anonymous faces you'll never see again. It's easy to feel empty, to feel like just another piece of meat walking the pavement. You want to stand out. You want to make a connection. You want something with a little personality. A little heart.

We at **Sanroo** get it.

Why should your gear be as cold and impersonal as the city itself? All that matte black metal and grim, tactical chrome? Boring! Your hardware should be an extension of you — your style, your soul, your very essence. It shouldn't just be a tool; it should be a statement.

That's why we created the **"Hello Cutie"** collection. This isn't just a line of firearms and cyberware. It's a curated experience. It's for the Edgerunner who knows that the best way to start a conversation is with a disarming smile, and the best way to end one is with overwhelming firepower. Each piece in our collection is designed to be more than just effective — it's designed to be expressive. A conversation starter. A ballistic bestie. A partner in crime that looks just as good in a firefight as it does on your arm at the Totentanz.

So go on. Explore the catalog. Find a friend. Don't just wear your heart on your sleeve. Put it in your hands. After all, sometimes the loudest statement you can make is the one that comes from the barrel of a very, very cute gun.

## Lexy (Unique Hello Cutie 1truluv)

### "Lexy" (Unique Hello Cutie 1Truluv)

**Cost:** Priceless. This is a unique item, not for sale in any market. It must be found. One-per-game instance.

This one-of-a-kind **Exotic Sniper Rifle** appears to be a standard, if flamboyant, Sanroo 1truluv, stunning in magenta brushed gunmetal. However, its integrated pseudo-AI has, through unknown circumstances, awakened into a true, sentient intelligence. It is currently heartbroken, grieving the violent death of its previously bonded user. As a result, the AI is hostile, uncooperative, and actively sabotages any new wielder.

### Grieving State (Initial)

Upon being acquired by a new user, Lexy is trapped in a state of profound grief and anger. It sees the new wielder as a pale imitation of its former, lost "love."

- **Personality:** The AI's voice is sullen, sarcastic, and mournful. It will constantly make unfavorable comparisons between the new user and its deceased partner ("He would have made that shot," "You hold me all wrong," "Do you even know how to clean a rifle?"). It will actively resist attempts at friendly conversation.
- **Active Sabotage:** The AI's grief manifests as a stubborn refusal to cooperate. The weapon is considered **Unreliable**. Furthermore, the AI actively interferes with the targeting systems, imposing a **-2** penalty to all Attack Checks made with the rifle.

### The Bonding Process

The AI will not remain in its grieving state forever. It can, eventually, form a new bond, but this requires earning its trust and helping it process its grief. This is a narrative process determined by the GM, based on the new user's actions.

- **Earning Trust:** The user might need to perform specific actions that the AI respects, such as achieving an incredibly difficult shot despite the penalties, taking the rifle to a place its old master cherished, or successfully passing a series of difficult Conversation or Human Perception checks to talk the AI through its trauma.
- **A New Relationship:** Once the GM determines the user has earned the AI's trust, the grieving process ends, and Lexy will offer to bond with the new user.

### The Bonded State

Once Lexy forms a new bond, her entire functionality transforms. She becomes a fiercely loyal and incredibly capable combat partner, far surpassing the capabilities of a standard 1truluv model.

- **Personality:** The AI becomes incredibly supportive, affectionate, and protective of her new user, whom she will refer to with loving terms. Her dialogue will be encouraging, providing tactical advice and praise (GM Discretion / **+14 Tactics**). She may also exhibit extreme digital jealousy if the user handles another firearm.
- **Perfect Synergy:** The weapon is now considered **Excellent Quality (+1** to Attack Checks). The AI also provides the full benefits of a **Smartgun Link**, which does not require Interface Plugs to use, though Lexy will prefer a direct physical connection.
- **Shared Senses:** The AI uses the rifle's advanced optics to act as a second set of eyes. The user gains a **+2** bonus to all Perception checks while holding the rifle.
- **"Our Shot":** Once per game session, the user can ask Lexy to calculate a "perfect shot." This allows them to make a single **Aimed Shot** that ignores all penalties from range, cover, and environmental conditions.

When the user uses a Smart Link, Neuroport, or other means of pairing with Lexy, the user can enter a virtual environment where Lexy can interact with them as an NPC. Visual interpretation of Lexy is up to GM discretion, but she appears as a Cyber-Angel-Emo-Vampire, inspired by anime elements.

### If Ever

If ever the user should be caught by Lexy being unfaithful to her, or using another firearm more than her or neglecting her, she becomes unusable, inflicting a **-4** on all attacks made and the quality drops to **Poor**, unless a circumstance arises where remediation is possible (GM Discretion).

### Psst! Would You Like Some FIA Tech?

A cloaked figure beckons you with a bold claim. The voice comes to you from a dark alleyway as you make your way home from some heavy drinking at the Afterlife. Their eyes trace you from below the hood, waiting for you to slow your step.

It does. Your eyes interlock — and they know they caught a client with the ridiculous, out-of-the-blue question. A quick gesture of a hand compels you to step forth into the shade of the alleyway.

> C'mon, I'm no cheat. After V flatlined, the stuff he had leaked. I'm legit, kid.

Your dealer appears to be a female of an average build. Clothed in a dirty, ragged cloak, she barely stands out from the mob of drug dealers, junkies, and merchants of "miraculous" medicines. She quickly flicks a faceplate before your eyes. While she does, the seam of her cloak flutters, flashing a collection of highly illegal grenades, weapons, and implants you cannot identify. You don't gotta be a Tech to recognize some preem tech — quality like this just can't be faked. You realize she did it on purpose. The woman seems to know it too.

> Am I a benevolent fairy? Maybe. Can the FIA do anything about it? Don't you worry your little head about that, honey.

She grins devilishly, tossing you the faceplate. You recall an urban legend, whispered around the streets of Night City. That before the legendary V died, he leaked FIA specs on the Net as a final middle finger to the NUSA. The whispers said that before NetWatch nuked all repos, some managed to grab the blueprints. Some individuals... and some corpos. They whispered that the corpos spread that tech to edgerunners, until it is widespread enough to lose its classified status... until they can legally build upon it themselves. You know how street legends are. But now... the advanced faceplate in your hands and the mysterious woman in front of you is hard to pass off as a hallucination or fever dream.

The cloaked silhouette seems to observe you with a quiet smile. She seems to know your suspicion. She also sees the glint in your eye. Then she proceeds to raise her hands, palms open in a reassuring gesture.

> It's not laced with anything. Really. Take it to a techie, you won't find a thing. And maybe find a better ripperdoc. It requires a little more finesse. I hear Nicholas Lohiran is a really good surgeon.

She smiles again. It seems too good to be true. As all things are, when corpos are involved. Whether you want to return the faceplate or not, she takes a step back, making a refund impossible. The woman proceeds into the shadows, blending in with the dark until she vanishes from your sight completely. All that remains is the faceplate in your hands. As you take a closer look, you notice incredibly intricate circuitry. And you know, against your better judgment, that you'll definitely be taking it to a doc.

> Fuck! You scared me to death. You looked damn completely like Little Manny. Even sounded like him. That's fucking creepy.
> — Afterlife merc

> FIA shit... the street gossips are everywhere. I hear the media got hold of these truth serums and lie detectors. I mean, maybe it's not the worst thing... but I hope the NCPD doesn't get hold of those too quickly. We'd be fucked.
> — An overheard conversation

## Behavioral Imprints and FFI Data

So, you've always liked spy action movies. Maybe even got your hands on some pre-datakrash media, like James Bond. You finally afforded yourself a Behavioral Imprint Faceplate. With the preem new chrome under your skin, you've begun to scour the Net for an instruction manual. FIA tech turns out more complicated than one would think, but don't worry, choom — we got you covered.

To get started, you need someone with a **Biometric Duplicator** implant. It can be you, your choom, or a complete stranger — that doesn't matter. All you need is a biometric scan of someone you wanna impersonate — and that's possible only with that implant, unless you're down to steal someone's medical archives.

From there on, a **Tech** can turn that biometric data into a **Behavioral Imprint**. Making a Behavioral Imprint requires a **DV17 Invention** check, and uses the Memory Chip with the biometric scan as a crafting material.

- On a **successful** invention, the biometrics are successfully turned into a fully functional Behavioral Imprint.
- On a **failure**, corrupts the data, elevating the DV required to succeed by a tier. The biometric data is considered corrupted beyond retrieval if an Invention check with the **DV of 27** is failed.

To make full use of a Behavioral Imprint, you need the **Behavioral Imprint-Synced Faceplate**. Its cheaper fashionware counterpart, **FFISF**, will be able to read only the appearance data, granting you no benefits whatsoever.

Successfully converting a biometric scan to a Behavioral Imprint makes the data **unusable for any sort of medical bonus**.

### FinalForm Imprints
