# Lifestyles

Reference for the four Lifestyle tiers in Cyberpunk RED. Pay at the start of each month. You get one week grace, then a Death Save each unpaid day. Living beyond your paid tier — the GM charges separately.

**Month length:** 28 days (4 weeks) for all per-day and per-week tallies below.

## How lifestyles work

- One monthly payment covers everything in your tier’s breakdown. You do **not** pay extra for items listed in that breakdown.
- Anything **beyond** the breakdown comes out of pocket at normal prices.
- Lifestyles are named after food but cover food, Agent service, entertainment, and transportation.
- **Housing is separate** from Lifestyle — see [Housing (p. 377–380)](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/16%20The%20New%20Street%20Economy.md#housing).

## Extrapolated food costs per meal

Homebrew math. Takes each lifestyle’s **monthly price**, subtracts every **book-listed non-food** cost for that tier (28-day month), and solves for what each **meal type** must cost if the remainder is food. Good Prepak and Fresh Food have no shelf price; those two are **chained** off the tiers below.

### Food vs. not food

| Counts as **food** | Counts as **not food** (subtract at retail) |
|-------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Daily lifestyle meals (Kibble, Generic Prepak, Good Prepak, Fresh Food) | BC entertainment budgets (movie, BD, concert slots, etc.) |
| [Koff Pops](../source/Source%20Text/Interface%20RED%20Vol%202/05%20Cargo%20Containers%20and%20Cube%20Hotels.md) (Kibble tier; 10eb snack — stays in food pool) | Taxis ([20eb](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/16%20The%20New%20Street%20Economy.md#services-and-entertainment) each) |
| Weekend **restaurant** sit-down ([20eb](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/16%20The%20New%20Street%20Economy.md#services-and-entertainment) — Generic tier) | Weekend **bar** drinks ([10eb](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/16%20The%20New%20Street%20Economy.md#services-and-entertainment) — Generic tier, if taken instead of restaurant) |
| World-class dinner ([500eb](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/16%20The%20New%20Street%20Economy.md#services-and-entertainment) — Fresh tier; same slot as BC’s monthly 500eb entertainment line) | [Elflines Online](../source/Source%20Text/Interface%20RED%20Vol%201/05%20Elflines%20Online.md) subscription (20eb; Generic Prepak+) |
| | [Glucose-Rich Brain Solution](../source/Source%20Text/Interface%20RED%20Vol%203/11%20Going%20Metal.md) (100eb; Good Prepak+ — supplement, not a meal) |
| | Hotel night ([100eb](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/16%20The%20New%20Street%20Economy.md#services-and-entertainment) — Fresh tier flavor) |
| | Agent connectivity, public transit, car rentals, SmartOven service — **no listed price** |

CP:R flavor that **duplicates** a BC slot is not subtracted twice (e.g. Kibble’s monthly movie/braindance = BC’s 20eb entertainment line; Good Prepak’s concert ≈ BC’s monthly 100eb Premium slot).

### Step 1 — Non-food subtraction (28-day month)

| Lifestyle | Price | Entertainment | Taxis | Other non-food | **Non-food total** | **Food pool** (price − non-food) |
|-----------|------:|--------------:|------:|---------------:|-------------------:|---------------------------------:|
| **Kibble** | 100eb | 20eb (1×) | 20eb (1×) | — | **40eb** | **60eb** |
| **Generic Prepak** | 300eb | 80eb (4×/wk) | 80eb (4×/wk) | 20eb (ELO) | **180eb** | **120eb** |
| **Good Prepak** | 600eb | 660eb (28×20 + 100) | 560eb (28×) | 100eb (Glucose) | **1,320eb** | **−720eb** |
| **Fresh Food** | 1,500eb | 2,300eb (28×50 + 4×100 + 500) | 560eb (28×) | 100eb (hotel) | **2,960eb** | **−1,460eb** |

At Good Prepak and Fresh Food, book-listed non-food **exceeds** the lifestyle price. That matches [BC p. 130](../source/Source%20Text/Black%20Chrome/10%20Economics%20101.md#lifestyles-in-detail): bulk contracts and status discounts — you are not paying retail for taxis, entertainment, or connectivity at those tiers. **Direct subtraction cannot price Good Prepak or Fresh Food meals.** Use the marginal chain below.

### Step 2 — Kibble & Generic Prepak (direct residual)

**Kibble** — 60eb food pool ÷ 56 kibble meals (2/day × 28 days):

$$\text{Kibble} = 60 \div 56 \rightarrow \mathbf{1eb/meal}$$

Shelf [Kibble Pack](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/16%20The%20New%20Street%20Economy.md#kibble-pack): **10eb** → ~**90% below retail** (lifestyle bulk rate).

**Generic Prepak** — 120eb food pool for 28 kibble + 56 generic prepak meals. Hold kibble at 1eb from the tier below:

| Line | Math |
|------|------|
| Kibble portion | 28 × 1 = 28eb |
| Remainder for generic prepak | 120 − 28 = 92eb |
| Per generic prepak meal | 92 ÷ 56 → 2eb |

$$\text{Generic Prepak} = (120 - 28) \div 56 = \mathbf{2eb/meal}$$

Shelf [Bags of Prepak](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/16%20The%20New%20Street%20Economy.md#night-market-generator): **20eb** → ~**92% below retail**.

**Generic weekend outing (CP:R flavor):** each weekend you get a good bar **or** a good restaurant — not both.

| Choice | Effect on the 120eb pool |
|--------|--------------------------|
| **Restaurant** (4 × 20eb = 80eb food) | Fits inside lifestyle; tight but the 120eb pool is for **home** meals only — weekend restaurant is additional food value on top |
| **Bar** (4 × 10eb = 40eb non-food) | Subtract 40eb → 80eb home food pool → generic prepak drops to **1eb/meal** |

Default chain uses the **120eb home-meal pool**; weekend food/bar is extra value not baked into per-meal rates.

### Step 3 — Good Prepak & Fresh Food (marginal chain)

When the tier above already pins lower meal types, the **price increase** between lifestyles pays for the meal upgrade:

$$\Delta\text{food budget} = \Delta\text{lifestyle price} + \text{value of meals removed}$$

**Generic Prepak → Good Prepak** (+300eb)

| | Generic Prepak tier | Good Prepak tier | Change |
|--|--------------------|--------------------|--------|
| Daily meals | 1 Kibble + 2 Generic Prepak | 1 Generic Prepak + 2 Good Prepak | −28 Kibble, −28 Generic Prepak, +56 Good Prepak |

| Line | Math |
|------|------|
| Meals removed (at solved rates) | 28 × 1 + 28 × 2 = **84eb** |
| Lifestyle increase | **+300eb** |
| Budget for new Good Prepak meals | 300 + 84 = **384eb** |
| Per Good Prepak meal | 384 ÷ 56 = 7eb |

$$\text{Good Prepak} = (300 + 28 + 56) \div 56 = \mathbf{7eb/meal}$$

No shelf price in CP:R or BC. ~**3.5×** generic prepak at lifestyle rates (~**3×** below generic’s 20eb shelf if Good Prepak were sold retail at that multiple).

**Good Prepak → Fresh Food** (+900eb)

| | Good Prepak tier | Fresh Food tier | Change |
|--|-----------------|-----------------|--------|
| Daily meals | 1 Generic Prepak + 2 Good Prepak | 1 Good Prepak + 2 Fresh Food | −28 Generic Prepak, −28 Good Prepak, +56 Fresh Food |

| Line | Math |
|------|------|
| Meals removed (at solved rates) | 28 × 2 + 28 × 7 = **252eb** |
| Lifestyle increase | **+900eb** |
| Budget for new Fresh Food meals | 900 + 252 = **1,152eb** |
| Per Fresh Food meal | 1,152 ÷ 56 → 20eb |

$$\text{Fresh Food} = (900 + 56 + 196) \div 56 = \mathbf{20eb/meal}$$

No shelf price. Coincidentally near **Bags of Prepak shelf (20eb)** — extrapolated lifestyle fresh food ≈ what generic prepak costs **à la carte**, which fits the setting: real food is a luxury, prepak is the mass-market upgrade.

### Step 4 — Results

| Food type | Implied lifestyle cost/meal | Book shelf price | Discount vs shelf |
|-----------|----------------------------:|-----------------:|------------------:|
| **Kibble** | **1eb** | 10eb | ~90% |
| **Generic Prepak** | **2eb** | 20eb | ~90% |
| **Good Prepak** | **7eb** | — | — |
| **Fresh Food** | **20eb** | — | — |

Rounded to whole eb. Shelf comparison only where CP:R lists a purchasable meal ([gear p. 352–354](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/16%20The%20New%20Street%20Economy.md#master-gear-list)).

### Step 5 — Cross-check (does it close?)

Plug extrapolated meal prices back into each lifestyle’s **daily meal mix**. Non-food is **not** charged at retail here — only food is tallied.

| Lifestyle | Price | Food at extrapolated rates | Left for non-food (implicit subsidy) | Retail non-food if bought separately |
|-----------|------:|---------------------------:|-------------------------------------:|---------------------------------------:|
| **Kibble** | 100eb | 56 × 1 = **56eb** | **44eb** | 40eb (matches at retail; 4eb slack in food pool) |
| **Generic Prepak** | 300eb | 28×1 + 56×2 = **140eb** | **160eb** | 180eb (rounded rates overshoot 120eb pool slightly) |
| **Good Prepak** | 600eb | 28×2 + 56×7 = **448eb** | **152eb** | 1,320eb → **~1,170eb subsidized** |
| **Fresh Food** | 1,500eb | 28×7 + 56×20 = **1,316eb** | **184eb** | 2,960eb → **~2,780eb subsidized** |

The bottom two tiers spend most of the lifestyle payment on **food** at these rates; taxis, entertainment, glucose, and hotel are largely **subsidized** — consistent with BC’s note that lifestyle benefits can exceed the sticker price.

**Ratio check between tiers:**

| Step up | Price jump | Implied jump per “main” meal | Multiple |
|---------|----------:|-----------------------------:|---------:|
| Kibble → Generic Prepak | +200eb | 1 → 2 | 2× |
| Generic → Good Prepak | +300eb | 2 → 7 | ~3.5× |
| Good → Fresh Food | +900eb | 7 → 20 | ~3× |

Each tier is a steep jump in food quality/cost; Fresh Food’s +900eb is the largest step, matching CP:R’s 600 → 1,500eb gap.

## Mr. Biscuit vs lifestyles — outskirts survival economics

[Everest VentureWare Mr. Biscuit Multi-Food Processor](../source/Source%20Text/Black%20Chrome/06%20General%20Gear.md#mr-biscuit-multi-food-processor) — **500eb (Expensive)** · [BC p. 49](../source/Source%20Text/Black%20Chrome/06%20General%20Goods%20and%20Gear.md#mr-biscuit-multi-food-processor)

### What the book says

> Everest VentureWare developed the Mr. Biscuit Multi-Food Processor with **increasingly common food scarcity** in mind. Any organic material can be fed into the machine for processing — hay, grass, leaves, squirrels, woodchips, and more. After **ten minutes** it prints green wafers. Despite somehow **tasting worse than Kibble**, the wafers are **filling and nutritious**.
>
> Using a Mr. Biscuit, a **group of up to 10 people** can **survive without paying for a Lifestyle** so long as they live in a location with an **Outskirts Threat Rating**. There **isn't enough organic material** to feed a Mr. Biscuit regularly **inside a city**.

Also bundled on the [Zacatzontli pickup truck](../source/Source%20Text/Black%20Chrome/08%20Vehicles.md) (27,000eb Super Luxury) with AirWell 50s and a WorldSat sphere — a deliberate **nomad/outskirts survival package**.

### What that implies about the world

| Theme | Reading |
|-------|---------|
| **Scarcity** | Food insecurity is normal post-War; corps sell Kibble/Prepak in cities, but the **Outskirts still have biomass** (plants, vermin) if you can process it. |
| **Urban vs rural** | Night City and similar zones are **organically starved** for Biscuit purposes — you cannot opt out of the food economy downtown. Continental Brands [Oasis markets](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/15%20Everyday%20Life.md#kibble) own the shelf; Mr. Biscuit is the **off-grid bypass** for Nomads and perimeter crews. |
| **Class** | [Fresh Food](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/15%20Everyday%20Life.md#fresh-food) is elite city luxury (gardens under siege, Nomad cattle drives, Corp deals). Mr. Biscuit is **prole/scavenger** tech — nutritious, ugly, free inputs. |
| **Taste vs calories** | Wafers are **below Kibble** on flavor but **meet survival needs**. Quality is not Good Prepak or Fresh Food; it is “you do not starve.” |
| **Lifestyle ≠ food only** | The book says survive **without paying for a Lifestyle** — not without paying for **housing**, threat exposure, or gathering labor. You skip the **100–1,500eb food-and-services bundle**, not rent or danger. |

### Where Mr. Biscuit sits on the food ladder

```
Mr. Biscuit wafers  <  Kibble  <  Generic Prepak  <  Good Prepak  <  Fresh Food
     (taste)              ↑ extrapolated 1eb/meal at lifestyle rates
```

Nutritionally: **≈ Kibble tier** (survival-sufficient).  
Experientially: **worse than Kibble** — no Prepak heat-and-eat, no restaurant outings, no real tomatoes.

### Hypothetical: Fresh-tier food processor (homebrew)

*Not in the books.* Math for a device that feeds 10 people **Fresh Food–equivalent nutrition** the way Mr. Biscuit feeds **Kibble-tier** nutrition.

#### Step 1 — Nutrition tier ratio

Assign Mr. Biscuit output = **Kibble lifestyle food value** (**1eb/meal** extrapolated). Target = **Fresh Food meal value** (**20eb/meal**).

$$\text{Nutrition multiplier} = \frac{20}{1} = \mathbf{20\times}$$

Cross-check via monthly food pools (1 person, 28 days):

$$\frac{1{,}316}{60} \approx \mathbf{22\times} \qquad \frac{1{,}500}{100} = \mathbf{15\times} \text{ (full lifestyle sticker)}$$

Use **~15–22×** depending on whether you scale on extrapolated food-only or full lifestyle price.

#### Step 2 — Device cost (linear scale on Mr. Biscuit)

| Method | Formula | Result | CP:R price band |
|--------|---------|-------:|-----------------|
| **Meal-value ratio** | 500 × (20 ÷ 1) | **10,000eb** | Super Luxury |
| **Food-pool ratio** | 500 × (1,316 ÷ 60) | **~11,000eb** | Super Luxury |
| **Lifestyle sticker ratio** | 500 × (1,500 ÷ 100) | **7,500eb** | Luxury / Super Luxury |

**Homebrew range: ~7,500–11,000eb** for a Mr. Biscuit–sized unit (10 people) at Fresh nutrition — same band as a [Compact Groundcar](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/09%20Friday%20Night%20Firefight.md) (30,000eb) or high-end cyberpets. Call it **10,000eb (Super Luxury)** round number.

Per-person buy-in (10-share): **~1,000eb** vs Mr. Biscuit’s **50eb**.

#### Step 3 — What changes besides price

Mr. Biscuit turns **any organics** (woodchips, grass) into survival wafers. Fresh Food in-setting means **real produce and meat** from [guerrilla gardens, livestock, and Corp-guarded supply lines](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/15%20Everyday%20Life.md#fresh-food) — not lawn clippings.

| | **Mr. Biscuit** | **Hypothetical “Mr. Harvest”** |
|--|-----------------|--------------------------------|
| **Device cost** | 500eb (Expensive) | **~10,000eb (Super Luxury)** |
| **Nutrition tier** | ≈ Kibble (survival) | ≈ Fresh Food lifestyle |
| **Feedstock** | Free biomass (grass, squirrels, woodchips) | **Quality organics** — garden veg, livestock; book ingredients **~50eb** each at retail |
| **Location** | Outskirts only | Likely **secured farm / perimeter** — still not viable downtown ([same city rule as Biscuit](../source/Source%20Text/Black%20Chrome/06%20General%20Gear.md#mr-biscuit-multi-food-processor)) |
| **Corp politics** | Ignored by Continental Brands | **Actively hostile** — bypasses Oasis / grower–Corp detente |
| **Processing** | 10 minutes → green wafers | Plausible **longer cycle** + **filtering** (CP:R fresh food often has [radioactivity / toxin traces](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/15%20Everyday%20Life.md#guerrilla-gardening)) |

#### Step 4 — Ongoing cost (feedstock is not free)

If Fresh-tier output needs **real ingredients** instead of hay:

| Model | 10 people, 28 days (1,120 fresh-equivalent meals/month) | Monthly feedstock |
|-------|----------------------------------------------------------|------------------:|
| **Lifestyle-equivalent** (extrapolated) | 56 fresh meals × 10 people @ 20eb | **11,200eb** |
| **Shelf ingredients @ 50eb** (fruit/veg/chicken) | 1,120 × 50eb | **56,000eb** — worse than paying Fresh lifestyle |
| **Guerrilla garden** (labor + security) | 0eb cash, raid risk | **0eb** + campaign cost |

A Fresh-tier processor only makes economic sense with a **protected food source** (Nomad farm, Shroomer tunnel, crew garden) — same fiction as Mr. Biscuit needing Outskirts biomass, but **harder** because rivals [kill for cabbages](../source/Source%20Text/Core%20Rulebook/15%20Everyday%20Life.md#killing-for-cabbages).

#### Step 5 — Break-even vs buying Fresh Food

| | **Mr. Harvest (~10,000eb)** | **10 × Fresh Food lifestyle** |
|--|----------------------------:|--------------------------------:|
| Upfront | 10,000eb | 0eb |
| Monthly food (extrapolated) | ~0eb* + amortization | ~13,160eb food / **15,000eb** full |
| Break-even | 10,000 ÷ 15,000 ≈ **< 1 month** (10-person crew) | — |

\*Assumes secured free feedstock like Biscuit; if you pay 50eb ingredient retail, **never** breaks even.

#### Summary formula (homebrew)

```
Fresh-tier processor cost  ≈  Mr. Biscuit cost × (Fresh meal extrapolated ÷ Kibble meal extrapolated)
                           ≈  500eb × (20 ÷ 1)
                           ≈  10,000eb  (Super Luxury)

Fresh meal extrapolated cost     =  20eb/meal  (no book shelf price)
Fresh food-only lifestyle/month  ≈  1,316eb/person  (28-day month)
```

**Bottom line:** Fresh Food nutrition is **~15–22×** more valuable per meal than what Mr. Biscuit provides. A processor that delivers that tier is not a 500eb camping gadget — it is **~10,000eb** super-premium hardware that still needs a **defended farm**, not city scrap, and Continental Brands would treat it as a threat to the food economy.
