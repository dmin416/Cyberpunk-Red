## Screamsheet Generator

Screamsheets! In-game, they befuddle, bemuse, and bedazzle the public of Night City by providing entertainment, local gossip, and, on occasion, actual news! Out-of-game, they act as tools, allowing the GM to pass along important information, provide context, advance plot lines, show the consequences of actions, and explore the world outside of the campaign.

Of course, not everyone has a degree in journalism to help them write in-world articles. The Screamsheet Generator is here to help. Use it to randomly (or not so randomly) generate articles you can hand to your Players with just a bit of fill-in-the-blanks action, or use it to create base-level articles you can transform, twist, and change to fit the concept in your mind.

### Using the Generator

At its core, this generator consists of a collection of tables designed to allow you to create random content by rolling dice and combining the results. However, if you see an element that seems like a particularly good fit for your game, skip the die roll and grab it! The Screamsheet Generator is a tool to enhance your game. Use it however works best for you and your Players!

The Screamsheet Generator works by directing you through a collection of tables. At each step, you will select a piece of content. Be sure to copy these down as you go. Then, complete any fillable fields, denoted by brackets (Example: [Name 1]), polish any rough areas, and create a handout to share with your group!

Here's the process.

**Step 1: Type of Article**

The first thing you'll select is the type of article you're creating. Do you want a news flash? An entertainment piece? A help wanted ad? The format you choose will determine which content tables you use to create the article. Use Table A to select the type of article you want to create.

**Step 2: Content Paragraphs**

There are different sets of content tables for each type of article listed in Table A. Once you've selected the type of article you're creating, turn to the relevant content tables and roll once on each table to select your article's intro, body, and conclusion. Some paragraphs may work better together than others, so don't hesitate to reroll or rewrite some of the text to create a better final article.

To make these screamsheets modular, proper nouns and key details have been left for the user to fill in. These fillable fields are in brackets (Ex: [Name 1]) and will be dealt with during Step 4.

**Step 3: Headline**

Readers begin an article by reading the headline but most journalists pick their headline after the article is written. This is because, in reality, headlines have more to do with how an incident resolves than with how it begins. The headline is, therefore, tied to the conclusion paragraph. Once you've selected a conclusion, the instructions in the generator will point you to a choice of headlines.

**Step 4: Filling in the Details**

Now it's time to fill in all the blanks — those fields in brackets in your article. You can select people, places, or things from one of the many Cyberpunk RED books and DLCs, grab a proper noun from your own campaign, or make up something on the spot! This is where you personalize the article to fit your table.

Where a fillable field indicates a part of speech (Ex: "[Adjective]"), you can either choose a word yourself, or, for a bit of levity, you can ask your players to supply the missing word as if the article was part of an improvisational party game.

**Step 5: Polish**

You've selected your content paragraphs. You've chosen a headline. You've filled in any missing details. The last step is to read your article from start to finish to make sure it makes sense.

It's important to give each article a final read from start to finish. While the pieces of each style of article are designed to fit together, the random nature of this generator means that sometimes it may generate something that doesn't make a lot of sense. Remember that the paragraphs provided here are just building blocks to enhance your game. Don't hesitate to reroll or replace any part of the article that doesn't fit well, or that doesn't fit the narrative you're trying to tell!

### How It Works: An Example

Kerry, the GM, decides they'd like to generate a screamsheet article to add some depth to their game.

Kerry wants to emphasize that, while the players have been focusing on their own missions, bad things have been happening elsewhere in the city, so they decide to skip rolling on Table A and use the "News Flash" tables to generate their article.

Kerry rolls a d6 on each content table to randomly choose the pieces of their article. They roll three times: once for the intro, once for the body, and once for the conclusion.

Kerry rolls a 1, a 2, and a 6, generating an article that looks like this ...

**Intro:** As our regular readers may recall, [Name 1], a prominent [Relationship Type] of [Group 1], was reported missing [Number] weeks ago after the [Incident] at [Location]. [Group 1] has offered a reward for any information leading to [Name 1]'s safe return, but little progress has been made since their disappearance.

**Body:** The situation got violent last night. Sources report that after an intense firefight with [Group 2], [Group 1] agents were seen hurrying a disheveled [Job Title] named [Name 1] out of the area. Our reporters attempted to follow the trail but lost track of the crew amid the busy streets.

**Conclusion:** Meanwhile, the situation went from bad to worse for locals when a [Adjective] drone stored somewhere in [Group 2]'s facility was activated. The machine rampaged through the area, crushing vehicles and bystanders caught in its path and bringing down the front wall of a nearby building before local lawmen were able to neutralize it.

When asked for comment, [Group 2]'s spokesperson merely said, "With innovation comes risk. We can only hope that the people of Night City realize how lucky they are to be on the cutting edge of such marvelous advancements."

[Group 2] has offered to compensate the victims of this most recent mishap, provided they can document their losses.

. . .

These pieces of text do make a coherent story, but beginning by talking about a missing person and ending with a paragraph about a rampaging robot seems a little disconnected to Kerry.

They decide to reroll the first paragraph. This time they get a 3, directing them to a new intro ...

**New Intro:** "We never could have planned for this," [Group 1]'s spokesperson commented in the wake of the recent disaster.

. . .

Kerry likes this intro better but decides it would make more sense for the group with the rampaging drone to be issuing the statement. They keep this intro but change [Group 1] to [Group 2] here. Kerry also notices that the transition from the intro to the body feels awkward, so they decide to remove the first sentence of the body.

Next Kerry needs a headline. They look at the note at the end of their conclusion paragraph, which reads, "NF4."

If they had chosen a different conclusion paragraph, Kerry might have been directed to choose between several different headlines, but this one is pretty straightforward. Kerry looks at the headlines for the "News Flash" articles and finds NF4 ...

**Stolen Drone Rampages Through [Location (Neighborhood)]!**

Kerry tweaks the headline to be more sensational, then adds it to the top of their article, leaving them with something that looks like this ...

### Finishing It Up

Once you've finished generating and tweaking your Screamsheet set it up to present to your Players. You can keep it low key and simple by placing the article(s) in a document and handing it over in your preferred format, but if you want to go the extra mile, download the Screamsheet Generator Template Pack. The Pack contains print-ready JPG images featuring two of Night City's most popular Screamsheet outlets: Night City Today News (a more formal and traditional news source) and The Augmented Optic (which reads more like a group of somewhat informative blog posts). You can pop the images into a word processor or image manipulation program and format your articles over the top before presenting your unique Screamsheet to your Crew on game night.

We recommend ½ inch (1.27 cm) margins on the left, right, and bottom. The gutters between the text and the header and between the columns should be ¼ inch (.635 cm). To match our fonts, follow these guidelines.

#### Night City Today News

We use a font named Futura FT specifically, but any Futura should do the job.

- **Headline Font:** Futura Bold (14 point, 13.5 leading)
- **Byline Font:** Futura Oblique (8 point, 8 leading)
- **Body Text Font:** Futura (11 point, 12.5 leading)
- **Link Font:** Futura Bold followed by Futura (10 point, 6 leading). Color is #3F4791

#### The Augmented Optic

We use a font named Video for the Augmented Optic, but any 80s CRT computer screen-style font will do.

- **Headline Font:** Video Bold (12 point, 13.5 leading)
- **Byline Font:** Video (8 point, 4 point leading)
- **Body Text Font:** Video (10 point, 14 point leading)
- **Link Font:** None. The Augmented Optic is an extremely low effort publication.
