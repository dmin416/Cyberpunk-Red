### Mr. Editor

By John "The Hoss" Havel Junior

Effective immediately, stop buying *Solo of Fortune* if you've never seen combat. This magazine isn't for you. *Solo of Fortune* is for professionals who put food on the table with hard-earned skills. It's where we publish letters from the who's who of modern warriors. It's a place of honor and respect for those who wield the power to dominate the battlefield. It isn't a zoo window from which to gawk slackjawed at the dangerous life of a mercenary. It's for Solos, by Solos. Attempting to duplicate what you read in this magazine will get you killed, likely by a reader of this magazine. So piss off, poser. This includes the staff of Puddleforge Games. Go find "gritty" material for your Cyborg Anarchist books somewhere else. Up your own ass, preferably.

#### ▶ GM Warning ◀

This content is above the power level of most Street Level campaigns. If you allow your Players to take items from *Solo of Fortune 2045*, you are increasing the power and/or complexity level of the campaign from street level to one more suited to telling mercenary stories. If you're aiming for street level, you may want to restrict these items in your campaign to a one-off storyline, or even disallow them entirely. You'll certainly need to field more difficult Hardened threats to challenge a party with these items. See the Hardened DLC series for more information.

#### ▶ Hot Tip ◀

To Harden an NPC in a hurry, increase all attacking and defending Skills by 2, and Tech Upgrade their armor to either reduce all of its armor penalties by 1 or increase SP by 1. Your Hardened NPCs shouldn't have Poor Quality items unless they're Mooks.

#### ▶ For Players ◀

If you equip items from *Solo of Fortune 2045*, your Edgerunner is immediately considered "Hardened", meaning your GM will field Hardened threats. The items in this DLC are a ticket straight to a tougher game.

#### ▶ It's All Optional! ◀

*Solo of Fortune 2045* adds new rules, new gear, and new complications. It has the potential of increasing the crunch level of your game, so remember, this is all optional. If it bogs down your game and dampens the fun, put it aside and try again later. This is especially true for newer gamers. We recommend trying out the basics of Cyberpunk RED before layering on new rules.

### Introducing Mercenary Level

#### What is the Mercenary Level?

Mercenary Level takes the action beyond The Street by focusing on tactical combat and free-form missions. It poses an increased challenge, but offers increased rewards. At the Street Level, Edgerunners are often hired to be criminals and finish jobs. At the Mercenary Level, Edgerunners are hired to complete objectives in intense missions.

#### How does a Mercenary Level Campaign differ from a Street Level Campaign?

Mercenary gameplay focuses on missions that the Crew is tasked to undertake, where planning and tactical gameplay are required from the start in order to succeed. The focus of a Mercenary Campaign shifts away from heists, which are better played at Street Level, and towards high-stakes combat encounters. The personality of the Cyberpunk world remains the same, but the ability to affect change within it is drastically changed. A successful mission at the Mercenary Level can easily tip the balance of power in a region, toppling factions and changing the status quo for better or worse.

#### Do all Roles fit in the Mercenary Level of play?

Yes, but you might need to be flexible, and even creative to explain their character's motivations for being there. The GM will have to be similarly flexible to explain how some Role Abilities might work in distant lands. Lawmen and Execs may have to work with local factions, for example. Just as in Street Level Campaigns, it's the responsibility of everyone at the table to work together to tell the story.

#### Mercenary Morality

There's room for moral choices in gigs on The Street. Crews can pick local jobs and work against the rich and powerful instead of for them. Mercenary Level doesn't offer the same level of flexibility. Most merc work is funded by corporations, governments, or the otherwise extremely wealthy. When you go merc, you risk selling your soul to the highest bidder.

#### Mercenary Level Balancing

Everything at Mercenary Level is Hardened – for more information on Hardened threats, see the Hardened DLC series. Beyond that, at the Mercenary Level, balance is fundamentally different. Instead of being balanced around Edgerunner count, at the Mercenary Level, the opposition the crew faces is determined entirely by the situation they find themselves in. Military bases don't magically contain fewer people because you've brought a small squad to assault them.

The quality of enemies the crew faces increases at the Mercenary Level as well. Hardened Lieutenants represent the average combatant you'll face at the Mercenary Level. Hardened Mooks are used sparingly to depict trainees and new hires. Hardened Minibosses and Bosses are saved for challenging encounters, climactic moments, and when encountering fierce resistance. The quality of the equipment, armor, and weapons your enemies equip themselves with also increases dramatically. The GM has full access to the weapons and items in this book. Expect the opposition to be equipped with military-grade gear.

As an additional bit of spice, at the Mercenary Level, the GM is encouraged to field Complete Package characters, complete with Role Abilities, to represent rival crews, with some further IP spent to juice them to the level of the crew. To underscore their equality to the players, Rival edgerunners included in a Mercenary Mission can even use LUCK. These rival crews are usually hired to stop or interfere with your Crew's ability to complete their Mission, or even to undercut them and steal the treasure/data/etc. right out from under them. They are a powerful way to introduce recurring NPCs that can show up down the road as rivals, or maybe even to show up as allies or just as a social encounter in a bar tucked away in a forgotten corner of the world.

Finally, the Mercenary Level of play is not balanced around looting everything you see at the end of your mission. Your objective is the mission, not stripping the enemy for parts. Besides, the spoils of war belong to your employer, not you. Have your fun on the battlefield, but you will be expected to check your gear and your loot into the armory when you return to base.

#### Mercenary Resources & Mission Rewards

Payouts from Mercenary Missions are higher than those from Street Level jobs, and come with an Operational Budget, which is given at the start of the job. The Crew can't keep anything that they buy with this Operational Budget after the Mission, including cyberware and upgrades for vehicles, weapons, and ACPAs. Anything they don't spend goes away. Your employer might have a Fixer to help source gear, but don't be afraid to call on your own contacts, too.

Many Missions take place far away from the Crew's home stomping grounds. While on such a Mission, characters do not need to pay rent, and once they're on site, the crew is typically given access to a Headquarters (see No Place Like Home DLC) until the job is complete. It's always the GM's call if travel is handwaved or a separate session's worth of unexpected adventure.

The Fixer's Role Ability works for negotiating increased pay for Missions the same way it does on Jobs. IP rewards remain the same, except that every Mercenary Mission you survive counts as at least a successful Mission for the purpose of assigning Group IP.

#### Mission Parameters

Gamemasters, use the following examples to develop your Mercenary Missions.

**"Easy" Mission**

Organized and armed resistance is expected. No mission can truly be considered easy.

"Destroy a convoy before it reaches a manufacturing facility. Eliminate all targets except the driver of the third car. An ACPA pilot, callsign Tyrant Wing, will attempt to intervene. Kill them."

- Operational Budget (per person): 1,000 to 5,000eb
- Mission Pay (per person): 3,000 to 4,000eb

**Typical Mission**

The enemy force is entrenched and far outnumbers our own, but the Crew has certain tactical advantages that will level the playing field.

"Participate in a night raid of a suspected resistance base in the mountains. Capture a member of their leadership to stand trial, we don't care which one. We expect to encounter heavy resistance if reinforcements have time to scramble, so act quickly. Should our intel be faulty and the facility turn out to be not resistance-allied, leave no witnesses."

- Operational Budget (per person): 2,000 to 7,000eb
- Mission Pay (per person): 4,000 to 7,000eb

**Dangerous Mission**

The enemy outnumbers the Crew, outclasses them, outguns them. Outmaneuvered, they grasp desperately at a slim chance of victory.

"Destroy Petrochem's three newly established research bases in the Blue Mountains within a two week time period. Do not wear or carry identifiers. If you are captured, we will deny all involvement."

- Operational Budget (per person): 10,000 to 30,000eb
- Mission Pay (per person): 6,000 to 9,000eb

### Under Extreme Circumstances

By "Captain" Olav "The Mechanic"

The Neo-Soviet air of the Barents Sea is icy in my lungs. I'm on an independently operated fishing and part time mine-sweeping boat stuffed full of Norwegian-born sailors. I am thousands of miles from land, and thousands of eurobucks in the hole in knockout gas for the vessel's crew, who are now sleeping soundly. I had put them under the impression I was chartering their fine vessel to do corporate espionage, to take some pictures of the oil rig poking over the horizon. The truth is that I'm here to take pictures with a bit more flash than they're used to. If I time this operation wrong, the volley of return fire won't be pretty, and a few of them might not wake up. The computerized aiming systems of the six Archimedes briefcase missiles I've laid out over every horizontal surface of the deck click, beep, and go green-green. It was hard enough to get this close without alerting SovOil's many ears in the area. There are a couple of ways this can go down, but I'm very sure I'm never getting another shot at this, and furthermore, I doubt anyone ever will. If SovOil's satellites were still up and properly aligned like they were twenty years ago, I wouldn't even be here. Times have changed. Plenty of these corporation's eyes are still blind from injuries sustained during the war. Weakness like this simply wasn't tolerated. I throw up a jammer and cover the gear with a ghostsuit tarp, waiting for a moment of tactical advantage.

I look out to the ocean. There's a storm coming in from the Arctic Circle, which should hit us in twenty minutes. That's good for the job, but introduces challenges. I'm serviceable behind the wheel of a ship, but an arctic storm easily stretches my abilities. I realize I'm going to have to wake one of the crew up. I pull the ripcord on one of my insurance policies, injecting Surge via Bodyweight Autoinjector. I won't have time to rest tonight. Looking over the sleeping crew, I choose a fourth mate, a good mix of talent and, in case things go poorly, a comparative lack of attachment to the rest of the crew. After dragging his body above deck, I lay him down on the floor of the helm room and uninstall his jawbone radio communicator unit. I'm again reminded that a tool hand is the most profitable cyberware a merc can install. I handcuff him to the wheel. Rainfall begins to hit the deck. The storm is rolling in.

Weapons check. Left side, Magnum Opus Hellbringer, Short Barrel, Midnight Arms Explosive Ammunition. Function, live. Left side, Kendachi Mono-Guard. Live. Right side, Magnum Opus Hellbringer, Long Barrel. Function, live. Emplacement, Arasaka WSSA Sniper System, Long Barrel, Custom Scope. Function, live. I dial in the scope for potential chase boats. There's little chance I'm hitting anything at this distance in storm conditions, but at least hostiles won't be either. SovOil likely won't even be able to get boats in the water — those will be the first Archimedes targets.

Time to wake up my wheel man. "Проснуться!" My cyberhand holds their mouth closed until they calm down. They choose to comply, making my job much easier. The rain is freezing cold. I take my other hand off my Hellbringer and relay instructions to the pacified fisherman.

Time to start the fireworks. I kill the jammer and remove the tarps. The Archimedes systems respond, clicking, pre-arming, lighting green. I start with the leftmost briefcase missile. Three seconds to arm. Fire. A good arc lands a clean impact on the oil rig, felling the radio tower, which crashes down onto the helipad. Lucky me. I move to the right. Three more seconds. The second explosion catches the boatbay, setting fire to the observation deck. Three more seconds. Lifeboats this time. The job is mostly over now. The next three missiles are easy to land on the southwest support column, cracking it but not finishing the job. I've only two left, which my employer told me are "special". I'd hoped not to need them, and to say I did. Could have made a bit more money. Whatever they are, they're covered in techprinted plastic, helical curled fins, and markers. Like a science fair project. As I load the first one into the briefcase, I see an incoming Neo-Soviet rocket launch from the inflamed oil rig. I bite down on my NeuTongue to activate my pain editor and swap the missile to manual control.

The incoming rocket explodes in the air. Skeet shooting my .50 cal rips my left shoulder out of its socket, but I'm alive. I stumble back to the briefcase to regain control of my missile, white-knuckling the joystick to pull it down into a hit on the upper deck. The experimental pilebunker bores down into the rig's lower accommodation deck, exploding with a heavy shockwave. I load the final missile into the Archimedes, sending it toward the crack in the southwestern support column. It lands square in the meat of the wound, ripping clean off the concrete leg of the colossus. It stumbles, metal wrenching into the ocean. Looking down, my arm is broken, not dislocated. My pain editor does its job.

Through the Arctic storm, my Hellbringer was persuasive enough to prevent mutiny once the crew began to wake up. I spent the night awake from the Surge. One-armed, with a pistol in hand, I daresay I felt a bit like a pirate.

When we docked in Vardø, my client was quick to transfer the money, and the fishermen were happy to see me gone. I'm just happy I didn't have to kill any of them. I wasn't getting paid enough for that.

### All New Attachments

The professional mercenary needs the tools of their job to be tuned exactly to their specifications. With these new attachments, you'll be ready for the Mercenary Level Missions that await you.

#### Ammunition Compatibility Internals

**Cost:** 100eb (Premium)  
**Eligible:** All ranged weapons that fire bullets or shells, except flamethrowers.

Forcing something where it's not meant to be isn't always a good idea, and hey, maybe there was a reason that you weren't supposed to chamber incendiary ammo in your plastic rifle. Stopping ideas like these from blowing up in your face is what gunsmithing wisdom and experience is for. Do it right, and things might end up better than you expected.

This Attachment doesn't require an Attachment Slot to install and can be installed in Exotic Weapons. Attaching this attachment requires a DV17 Weaponstech Skill Check. Only one Ammunition Compatibility Internals may be installed. Once installed, this attachment may not be uninstalled. This weapon can now fire all Non-Basic Ammunition of any Ammunition category (Heavy Pistol, Assault Rifle, etc.) it could fire before installation.

#### Reinforced String

**Cost:** 250eb (Expensive)  
**Eligible:** Bows, Crossbows

Switching to a less forgiving string allows you to put significantly more power behind each arrow. Kits can be purchased for the task.

This Attachment doesn't require an Attachment Slot to install and can be installed in Exotic Weapons. Attaching this attachment requires a DV17 Archery or Weaponstech Skill Check. Only one Reinforced String may be installed. Once installed, this attachment cannot be uninstalled. This weapon's damage increases by 1d6 to a maximum of 5d6 when its user is not in a Kyudo Stance (see Interface RED Vol 4 page 80). Unless installed in cyberware, this weapon must be fired with two hands, even if it was previously a one-handed weapon.

#### Sniper Rifle Rechamber

**Cost:** 300eb (Expensive)  
**Eligible:** Sniper Rifles

Rechambering a high caliber caseless weapon to an even higher caliber requires much more than drilling a wider hole. Headspace must be considered, and in many cases, the weapon's receiver needs extensive modification. Luckily, they sell kits for that.

This Attachment doesn't require an Attachment Slot to install and can be installed in Exotic Weapons. Attaching a Sniper Rifle Rechamber requires a DV17 Weaponstech Skill Check. Only one Sniper Rifle Rechamber may be installed. Once installed, this attachment cannot be uninstalled. This weapon's damage increases by 1d6, to a maximum of 6d6.

#### Compatibility Rail

**Cost:** 50eb (Costly)  
**Eligible:** All Exotic Ranged Weapons

Attaching an external scope rail to an unusual or unique weapon design requires certain considerations. The end result won't always look good, but with enough gunsmithing elbow grease, it'll work.

This Attachment doesn't require an Attachment Slot to install and can be installed in Exotic Weapons. Attaching this attachment requires a DV17 Weaponstech Skill Check. Only one Compatibility Rail may be installed at a time. This weapon gains an Attachment Slot that can only be used to install Scope attachments.

#### Range Table Modification

**Cost:** 100eb (Premium)  
**Eligible:** All Ranged Weapons, individual eligibility by weapon category.

Nothing stock is perfect, and that's why God invented barrel extensions. Screw one on and you've got a weapon designed to work at the range you want to be fighting in. These have been around for so long that you don't need a lick of talent to get one running on your favorite side piece. Just remember to carry a backup in case the battlefield isn't the exact size you preordained it to be.

This attachment doesn't require an Attachment Slot to install and may be installed in Exotic Weapons. Only one Range Table Modification may be installed at a time. While installed, the Range Table the weapon fires on is changed to match that of the Modification. The weapon's damage, ammunition types, and other particulars are unaffected.

### Autofire Heaven, Bullet Hell

#### New Weapon Category, New Autofire

**Guide to reading the new Weapon Listings**

In *Solo of Fortune 2045*, we're trying a new method of communicating the features of a weapon that's easier to read and reference at your game table.

- **Weapon Name:** The name of the weapon.
- **Cost:** The cost of the weapon, followed by its availability.
- **Flavor Text:** In-world lore and a description of the weapon.
- **Classification:** Weapon Quality and the class of the weapon for ammunition and attachment compatibility.
- **Skill:** The skill the weapon is fired with in Single Shot (if applicable).
- **Range Table:** The Range Table this weapon is fired with in Single Shot (if applicable).
- **Damage:** The damage dealt by this weapon.
- **ROF:** The rate of fire of the weapon.
- **Hands:** The number of hands the weapon is fired with.
- **Capacity:** The amount of ammunition held by this weapon.
- **Special Firing Modes:** Any Special Firing Modes the weapon has.
- **Concealable:** Whether or not the weapon is concealable.
- **Open Slots:** The number of open attachment slots remaining on the weapon.
- **Attachments:** Attachments already installed in the weapon.
- **Special Features:** Any special features the weapon has.

Machine Guns are a new weapon category with no "generic" version — each weapon is unique, though all use Assault Rifle ammunition unless otherwise stated.

For attachment compatibility purposes, Machine Guns will accept any attachments as if they were classified as an Assault Rifle. As part of this new category, we've updated two guns from the *Cyberpunk RED* core rulebook with new variants.

Alongside Machine Guns, we're also introducing two new types of autofire: Machine Gun and Machine Pistol. Each has their own row on the new Expanded Autofire Table (see page 113).

And now, without further adieu, your big guns.

I was an architect of much suffering that your readership have endured, those being veterans impacted by the Second Central American War. I, Annabel Cruz, herein provide an account of my actions regarding my role in the horrific human chemical weapons testing undertaken as a joint effort by Biotechnica and the US military on unknowing American soldiers.

In 2001, after graduating from Night City University with a degree in Pharmacology, I accepted a job offer to work for Biotechnica in a junior engineering role. It landed me in a botany laboratory, in which I sequenced plant genomes, looking for the next triticum vulgaris megasuavis. After two years of no results, I found myself forced to either accept a "promotion" and transfer to a newly formed confidential lab, or be fired. I regret saying yes, and I regret doing so enthusiastically. It was pathetic. At the time I told myself that the new role was a chance to save my career from failing before it ever had a chance to begin. In hindsight, this choice was my last chance to choose a path of righteousness. I chose poorly.

I was transferred to a black site in Colombia. It was an entirely Portuguese-speaking lab, staffed entirely by scientists from the Rio de Janeiro Biotechnica offices. In the Night City office's haste to get rid of me, they must have assumed I knew Portuguese, probably because those racist pricks never cared about me enough to ask. Or maybe they did, and thought it was funny. Being the only researcher that communicated using a translation chip, and an early 2000's chip at that, I quickly earned the nickname "Robota" from my new colleagues. Inevitably, the close contact environment of the black site bonded us into a team. Perversely, while I committed the worst crimes of my life, I was happy. Happy to be a part of something that worked, to be a part of a winning team on the cutting edge of science.

The goal of our cell, Columbia-2, was to develop chemical weapons synthesized from local plants that could be used to silently attack an entrenched enemy force. The reasoning was that by working with locally available plants, the supply side of a potential conflict could be made more efficient. Most nerve gases have a notoriously short shelf life, and this was another problem local production could solve. We saw ourselves as scientists for harm-reduction. We envisioned ourselves creating a kinder, gentler alternative to nerve and chlorine gas, something akin to a short-acting mustard gas that would have no lasting effects on the target. We were told that Columbia-1 was working on an airborne cleansing nanomachine that would serve as the critical second half to our work, one that would reverse the effects of our weapons. Together, the final product would be a beautiful synthesis of chemistry and biology — a beautiful, ethical, modern weapon of war. I now know Columbia-1 never actually existed. They lied to ease our conscience.

If I had to start again, what would I do? Maybe you can take this as advice, but I wouldn't recommend it — I'm a couple of years out of date. From street rat to street samurai, the only difference is standards. Professionals get paid. We don't work for respect, territory, or revenge. We work for money. Money buys territory, helps you forget revenge. Work brings respect, in adoration and in fear.

You're going to need to invest money to get started, because before you can make the big bucks, you'll need to survive your first year as a mercenary. That's a couple of gunfights a month, for twelve months.

Think economically. You're effectively a new business, so you need starting capital. Sell everything you own. Keep it in cash. If you can't pull at least 10k together, you aren't ready for the big time. Get rid of your SIN, your phone number. Move out of your place. Burn all documentation. Pay a Netrunner to burn your digital footprint. You can't do this halfway. Attachments get you killed. Buy a backpack and stuff it full of cash.

Here's what I'd spend it on. Bodysculpting comes first. You need a new face. It will make everything else easier. Bribe the ripperdoc to wipe all the files. Get a new wardrobe. Your goal is nondescript. Avoid boots, vests, anything with camouflage. Nothing you've ever seen an off duty cop wear. Nothing flashy or identifiable. You want to look forgettable, like an accountant. Break up with your ripperdoc anyway. Get a new one you've never met before. You won't believe how many people I know that got killed this way. It's four, and one of those was kind of a double. Rippers love money, which makes them vulnerable to bribes.

You need chrome, practical chrome. To operate full-time as a Solo, you need enhanced antibodies, some form of subdermal plating, anti-dazzle, nasal filters, and if you're smart, hardening for every cyberlimb you've got. Second priorities are muscle and bone lace, and reinforced joints for your limbs. One broken bone can turn a bad job into your last job. I got by with much less early on, sure, but the times have changed.

You need a new Fixer, too, because they're even worse than ripperdocs at keeping secrets. Get this into your head: your Fixer isn't your employer, and isn't your friend, they're a tool to get in contact with your next gig. I don't care how many times they say they're giving you a "friend price". They all have a line, beyond which profit matters more than loyalty. That's their real "friend price".

Secure a safe house in two different neighborhoods. Cargo containers with simple setups. Rent should run you about two grand a month for both. That's the cost of doing business. You get bonus points if both safehouses are in rival gang territories. Your new neighbors won't snitch on you to their own enemies, but just in case, never take the same route home twice.

Only take jobs that match your skill level. Every single "big job" you're offered is a big trap. You aren't special, or lucky. There's a reason more established Solos passed up on that job. That job is either a good old fashioned bad idea, the trouble isn't worth the fee, or word has gotten out that the employer wants someone they can kill off, because, no witnesses. Don't work for corporations that have been backed into a corner, or even worse, are attacking one of their own. Palace intrigue is strict no-go. Those clients are guaranteed to turn on you after you've cleaned their house, nine times out of ten. In a way, it's not like they're even lying, because their nature is almost always laid bare in the job description. If they're willing to betray their own people, they're willing to betray you. When these clients are smart, they like to remain anonymous. That's why you should do your research before accepting any job. Don't be a loose end.

When things are going according to plan, a good rifle is your lifeblood, so treat yours like a princess. You want something that isn't unwieldy in a hallway but can handle distance. For those times when your princess leaves you high and dry, you need a backup. It should excel at solving problems, up close and personal and should always stay with you, even after a search. A shotgun in your cyberarm is a classic for a reason. You want armor penetration and high ammunition count, because some of these problems are Full Body Conversions. Invest in multiples, in case you get disarmed, and don't get too attached. A gun can be like a fingerprint. Better to throw it in a smelter than be identified.

I'll leave you with one more piece of advice, something to think about once you've established your rep enough to turn down three or so "lifechanging opportunities" offered to you by your Fixer "buddy". There's one particular kind of job unique to our industry that's guaranteed to shorten your career; never take a job to kill a fellow Solo. You should be ready, though. In the case you meet someone that has, remember that neither of you are special, or lucky.

But maybe, if you've listened to this old man's advice, it'll be you writing an article like this in twenty years time, and not the choombatta hunting you.

—M.B.

### ACPA in the Time of the Red

ACPA, Assisted Combat Power Armor, are combat environment suits encased in Metalgear® plates built around a powered assist provided by a step-in Linear Frame, which enable their pilots to move them around despite their extreme weight. On the battlefield, they serve a narrow "siege" role. They are outclassed in speed and cost effectiveness per trooper by standard vehicles on open ground, but superior to them in narrow urban environments.

The cost of ACPA suits, while lower now than during the 4th Corporate War, remains a large barrier of entry for most small militaries and mercenary companies. Add this to the fact that improvements in modern firepower, especially that of railguns, have lessened the once-invincible protection provided by Metalgear® armor, ACPA find themselves at a precarious crossroads.

In the 2040s, they are simultaneously too expensive for mass warfare, too niche for many operations, and reliant on a skilled pilot to bring out their full potential. ACPAs are better served as a force multiplier — boogeymen in theaters of urban and irregular war capable of maneuvering like a soldier but fighting like a vehicle.

#### Bootup Sequence

Getting into an unoccupied ACPA is an Action. As part of this Action, a user can connect themselves to the ACPA using two installations of Interface Plugs and become the ACPA's Pilot. The vast majority of all ACPA are installed with Authorization Handshake Ports (see page 122) that will only accept "authorized users." Getting out of an ACPA that isn't destroyed is an Action. Forget passengers. An ACPA only has room inside for a single person.

#### Clamshell Protection

While inside an ACPA, the pilot's body is encased tightly in a clamshell of armor, and is protected from outside attack.

- It's a tight fit and since the pilot guides the movement of the suit with their own limbs, any Armor Penalties from armor worn still applies and stacks with penalties imposed by the ACPA. Skip the flak vest and bring something lighter.
- ACPA pilots are very much "locked in" their suits. They cannot make use of any cyberware installed in their body that needs to be popped up, plugged in, or otherwise physically engage with something outside of the suit to function.
- When piloting an ACPA, the pilot uses their own Skills just like they would outside of the suit. When a physical attack made from outside your suit would damage the pilot, their ACPA takes the damage instead, reducing its HP and SP instead of that of the person inside. The pilot's equipped armor does not lose SP when their ACPA's SP is ablated.
- An ACPA cannot be grappled or thrown by anyone with a BODY 14 or lower and is immune to choke damage. The pilot cannot be grappled, thrown, or choked while inside an ACPA that isn't destroyed.
- An ACPA's head and limbs are not considered weak points, and ACPA are immune to Aimed Shots. Additionally, since ACPA are designed to reinforce a frail human body, Martial Arts Attacks and Special Moves don't halve an ACPA's SP.
- ACPAs cannot suffer the effect of Critical Injuries. Instead, whenever a Critical Injury is scored on an ACPA the pilot suffers 5 damage directly to their HP due to neural feedback. This damage is reduced to 0 for FBC pilots, due to their unique physiology.
- ACPA protect their pilots and their cyberware in a sealed and oxygenated environment, making them immune to EMP effects. All of an ACPA's components, onboard weapons and cyberware are considered hardened (cannot be damaged or rendered inoperable by EMP effects like Microwaver pulses or Non-Black ICE Program effects) and cannot be set On Fire.
- An ACPA's sealed environment provides the pilot immunity to electrocution, toxic gases, fumes, and all similar dangers that must contact skin, be inhaled, or injected to affect the target. Automatic light and noise compensation makes the pilot immune to blindness, deafness, or other effects caused by dangerous loud noises or flashes of light, like those of a Flashbang, Shrieker, or Growler.
- The sealed environment of the ACPA protects its pilot from radiation, extreme weather and environmental conditions, is watertight for underwater use, and is even suitable for use in space. An ACPA's onboard oxygen tanks last up to 24 hours, and can be refilled in 1 hour via exposure to ambient air.

#### When an ACPA is Reduced to 0 HP

ACPAs are immune to Wound Threshold effects. An ACPA that is reduced to 0 HP is Destroyed and ceases to provide any protections to its pilot. A destroyed ACPA's pilot is also unable to use any of its Onboard Weapons/Cyberware, or use its Linear Frame. The one upside to your ACPA being Destroyed is that you don't need to use an Action to exit a destroyed ACPA … you simply walk right through the new big hole your enemy made in the side of it.

#### Linear Frame Core

All ACPA are built around a Linear Frame that the user can take advantage of while they pilot the ACPA. This increase in BODY does not affect the user's HP or change their Death Save. If a user has a more powerful linear frame than the ACPA they are piloting, their BODY remains at the higher value.

The arms of an ACPA are treated as cyberarms for the purposes of damaging cover, and the legs of an ACPA are treated as cyberlegs for the purposes of falling.

#### Wielding Weapons

An ACPA can wield weapons as if it were a human. All normal rules apply, except an ACPA can wield any two-handed weapon with one hand except for bows.

#### Onboard Weapons & Cyberware

In addition to wielding any weapons held in their ACPA's hands, the pilot of an ACPA may also make use of their ACPA's Onboard Weapons and Cyberware. Wielding onboard weapons doesn't take up the use of the ACPA's hands. Any weapon installed as an Onboard Weapon or wielded in an ACPA's hands is considered to be connected to the pilot via an appropriate number of Interface Plugs for it to fully function, and if it's an onboard weapon, it cannot be disarmed. In addition to any cyberware installed as Onboard Cyberware on an ACPA, all ACPA contain a sensor suite that operates like a Radar/Sonar Implant (see CP:R page 363) that has been modified to scan out to 100 m/yds.

#### Tuned to Perfection

ACPAs are designed with tradeoffs in mind for the battlefield roles that they seek to fill. ACPAs, by themselves, do not have a REF, DEX, or MOVE STAT. The pilot's are used instead. While piloting an ACPA, a user's REF, DEX, and MOVE may be positively or negatively affected by the suit.

#### Maximum Operation Time

ACPA control feeds are difficult on the pilot's senses, not to mention their brain. A pilot who spends more than 4 hours piloting an ACPA on a given day suffers a -1 penalty to all Checks until they complete a day of rest. FBC pilots and non-FBC pilots with Rank 4 or higher in Moto or Interface are better attuned to the stress and do not suffer this penalty.

All pilots, no matter their Role Ability or FBC status, who spend more than 8 hours piloting an ACPA on a given day suffer a -2 penalty to all Checks until they complete a day of rest.

#### No Bullet Dodging in an ACPA … Unless

A pilot can't dodge bullets in an ACPA unless they meet one of the following requirements. If they do, they can dodge bullets, even if they couldn't outside of an ACPA:

- The ACPA has a MOVE bonus of +3 or higher.
- The pilot has a REF 8 or higher after bonuses/penalties, a Reflex Co-Processor, and the ACPA has no REF Penalty.
- The pilot has a DEX 8 or higher after bonuses/penalties, and the ACPA has no REF, DEX, or MOVE Penalty.
- The pilot has a Combat Awareness Rank of 6 or higher.

#### Modular Mecha Repairs

An ACPA is too complicated to be Jury Rigged by a Tech. However, ACPAs are designed for heavy use and relative ease of repair. The DV and amount of time it takes to repair a damaged/destroyed ACPA is based on the amount of damage it has sustained and the Check is made using the Weaponstech Skill. On a failed roll, halfway through the repair, the mechanic realizes that they'll have to start the repair again from scratch. Whenever an ACPA is repaired, all of its Onboard Weapons/Cyberware are repaired, and the SP of its Metalgear® clamshell and its HP are restored to maximum.

### Building an ACPA

To build an ACPA, first select its ACPA Linear Frame, then its ACPA Shell, and then decide how the ACPA is tuned in Maneuverability, Hydraulics, Acceleration, and Weight. You are required to make a selection, A or B, for each of these four tunings. Every decision made when building an ACPA impacts its HP and its REF, DEX, MOVE Bonuses/Penalties, so keep track of each as you go.

Add up to 6 Onboard Weapons to the ACPA. You may install up to 6 pieces of cyberware in an ACPA. Additional Linear Frames and cyberware that grants/restores SP cannot be installed. You cannot install cyberware only available to FBCs in an ACPA. When installed in an ACPA, all cyberware installation prerequisites are ignored, and all cyberware installed is treated as paired. Adding cyberware with open slots does not increase the total number of pieces that can be installed.

To find the market Price and Availability of your ACPA, begin with a base of 4,000eb. Add the cost of the ACPA Linear Frame, the cost of the ACPA Shell, the cost of each Onboard Weapon and Cyberware, and then add an additional 500eb per Onboard Weapon and Cyberware. Your ACPA's final Availability Rating is equal to that of its most difficult to acquire component.

A Tech cannot upgrade, fabricate, or invent an entire ACPA. Instead, they must use their Role Ability to upgrade, fabricate, or invent each of an ACPA's components separately. The components should then be assembled using the following rules.

When fabricating an ACPA Linear Frame or ACPA Shell component, the cost is half the cost of the Frame or half the cost of the Shell, and is done with the Weaponstech Skill. Final assembly of an ACPA from loose components/disassembly of an ACPA into its loose components is a DV24 Weaponstech Check, and takes 24 hours. When assembling an ACPA, a Tech must spend a base fee of 2,000eb but does not have to spend 500eb per Onboard Weapon and/or Cyberware piece. Adding, removing, or swapping out any number of Onboard Weapons/Cyberware is a DV21 Weaponstech Check, and takes 24 hours.

On a failure, halfway through the process, the mechanic realizes they need to start again from scratch, but the components are not lost.

#### Example: Creating an ACPA

Jeremy decides to order a new ACPA from a manufacturer. First, he chooses an ACPA Linear Frame and an ACPA Shell. He selects a Sigma Frame that comes with 22 HP, BODY 12, and +1 MOVE, and a Salvaged Shell that comes with 14 SP, +2 HP, and -1 MOVE. Together, before tuning, his ACPA has 24 HP and 12 BODY. Its +1 and -1 MOVE cancel each other out.

Jeremy isn't done, however. He must tune the ACPA, selecting either Option A or Option B for each of the four tunings: Acceleration, Hydraulics, Maneuverability, and Weight.

- For **Acceleration**, he selects Option B, which comes with -1 DEX.
- For **Hydraulics**, he selects Option A, which comes with -1 SP.
- For **Maneuverability**, he selects Option A, which comes with -7 HP.
- For **Weight**, he selects Option A, which comes with +7 HP.

After Tuning, his ACPA has 13 SP, 24 HP, and -1 DEX.

Now comes Onboard Weapons and Cyberware. Jeremy decides on a Highland Defense Stickybomb Launcher valued at 1,300eb and a Skate Foot valued at 500eb. Since all onboard cyberware is automatically considered paired, Jeremy doesn't need two Skate Feet.

Jeremy now needs to calculate the price of his ACPA:

- Base Cost: 4,000eb
- ACPA Linear Frame: 2,000eb
- ACPA Shell: 1,000eb
- Highland Defense Stickybomb Launcher: 1,300eb
- Skate Foot: 500eb
- Additional Cost per Onboard Weapon (×1): 500eb
- Additional Cost per Cyberware (×1): 500eb

The final cost for Jeremy to buy this ACPA is **9,800eb**, and it is Luxury availability, as its hardest to acquire part (Sigma ACPA Linear Frame) is Luxury.

If Jeremy was a Tech and he wanted to build this ACPA himself, he first would have to acquire or fabricate all of its parts separately (fabricating the Frame and Shell would be half the listed cost), spend 2,000eb, and then succeed at a DV24 Weaponstech Skill Check that takes 24 hours. He doesn't have to spend 500eb per Onboard Weapon and Cyberware, because he's doing the work himself. On a failure, halfway through the process, he would realize he needed to start again from scratch, but the components would not be lost.

#### Gamemaster Notes

The situation in the ArushiMoshi Metroplex is complex. The European Space Council (ESC) has turned the region into a secret battlefield for months now to make way for a planned military invasion that would ultimately grant them full control of the Kilimanjaro Mass Driver. The ESC has pulled out every trick in the book to soften up Tanzania, including sabotaging communications, paying and pressuring journalists into publishing hit pieces, setting off large scale EMPs, and even resorting to terrorist attacks in popular shopping neighborhoods in the Metroplex.

The people of Tanzania have not been fooled by the European Space Council. Anti-ESC protests are a daily occurrence, and many who are in the streets are pushing the government to fully deny European countries access to the mass driver in response to their crimes. Existing hate groups are taking advantage of the protests to attack European immigrant communities in the Metroplex, which has resulted in confusing clashes between the Tanzanian military and "protesters."

##### The ArushiMoshi Metroplex

The Crew arrives at the Kilimanjaro International Airport in a military plane one month before the negotiations begin, and are asked to change into Tanzanian military fatigues. While wearing these fatigues, Edgerunners will be treated as extensions of the state — one not everyone spilling out into the streets of Tanzania is happy with.

In addition to the fatigues, each Edgerunner receives an official-looking signed letter of support from Ambassador Mwambe (a powerful social tool) and a Swahili language Skill Chip. The Marangu Military Base is a Headquarters with 12 beds, Lockup (1 cell), Medbay, Upgraded Training Area Workshop, and Workstation.

This month (two weeks in the city and two weeks at the base) is your opportunity as a GM to flex your improvisational skills and play in a setting that isn't Night City. Key to achieving the feel of being in a different country will be roleplaying a different culture, one less cynical than Night City, but still more cynical than our present day world, exploring the political unrest in the streets, and the different ways the Edgerunners are treated in and out of uniform, especially if they look or act "foreign."

Pull on all the Lifepath data that can stretch internationally, like Exec companies, Fixer and Media contacts, Nomad packs, and Rockerboy fanbases. If the Crew gets into combat, make sure it either ties back into the unrest or brings them in conflict with ESC-hired saboteurs.

##### During the Negotiations

During the week of the negotiations, there are three major attacks on the anti-air battery.

**Anti-Air Battery**

Remote-operated w/Combat Number 16 • 15 SP • 100 HP • EQ Rocket Launcher w/ Long Tube & 50 capacity. The emplacement can be retracted underground with 3 Actions. While retracted, it cannot be attacked or be damaged. Cannot be Jury Rigged during combat.

**The first attack** is a long distance assault on the facility, which seeks to damage the crew's ACPA suits from range. It also makes use of two attack helicopters and a Magus ACPA, hoping to bait the anti-air battery into use so that it can be targeted by the ACPA's railgun.

**The second attack**, which occurs 10 hours later on the following day, is a full frontal assault using the Militech Commando ACPAs and a portion of the Hardened Security Operatives in a Groundcar, decided upon by the GM. Before this attack, the Crew's position is reinforced by two military special operatives (Hardened Security Officers with Shotguns & Explosive Slugs). This is also when any outside backup, perhaps engaged during the period before the attacks, arrives to help.

**The third attack**, which occurs 3 days after the last, is a desperate operation that sends all remaining units, including the Bombardier ACPA, at the anti-air battery. The enemy begins to flee after all their ACPAs have retreated or been eliminated. If no enemy escaped the second battle alive/uncaptured, the enemy's morale was broken, and this third attack never comes.

If the Crew is victorious, they are offered Tanzanian citizenship, and however things go down, is paid and is returned to whatever non-European airport they wish.
