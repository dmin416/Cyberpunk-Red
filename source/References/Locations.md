## Zone Types & Threat Ratings

In 2045, locals refer to seven **zone types** rather than the old 2020 neighborhood map. Each area also carries a **Threat Rating** (Executive, Corporate, Moderate, Combat, or Outskirts). A region can mix ratings as it gentrifies or decays.

| Zone Type | Threat (typical) | Summary |
|-----------|------------------|---------|
| [Hot Zone](#the-hot-zone) | Combat | Ground zero of the Arasaka nuke; ruined towers, scavengers, residual radiation |
| [Rebuilding Urban Center](#the-rebuilding-urban-center) | Corporate / Moderate | Surviving core; cranes, megabuildings, flooding, constant construction |
| [Executive Zone](#the-executive-zone) | Executive | Gated community for Corporate elite |
| [Combat Zones](#the-combat-zones) | Combat | Gang rule; no reconstruction |
| [Overpacked Suburbs](#the-overpacked-suburbs) | Moderate / Combat | Tent cities and refugee sprawl on old Beavervilles |
| [Reclaimed Perimeter](#the-reclaimed-perimeter) | Outskirts | Abandoned towns reclaimed by Nomads and private security |
| [Open Road](#the-open-road) | Outskirts | Highways; Nomad convoys vs. road gangers |

| Threat Rating | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Executive** | Secure gated neighborhoods; ultra-rich; armed security; maglev to work |
| **Corporate** | Corp-patrolled; safe from Combat Zone chaos; different kind of danger |
| **Moderate** | Working-class areas; safer than Combat Zones; spillover violence common |
| **Combat** | Worst parts of the city; bodies don't get cleaned up |
| **Outskirts** | Outside city sprawl; road gangers and Nomads |

## The Hot Zone

**Threat Rating:** Combat

Former central Corporate Zone. Much was bulldozed into the Bay as fill; what remains is wrecked skyscrapers, burned vehicles, and entombed dead. Radiation has subsided but persists. Abandoned to the worst gangs — scavengers, suicides, and treasure-hunters only.

### Neighborhoods (Old Zones)

| Area | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Old Bank Block** | Old banks; mostly rubble; never officially cleared |
| **Old City Center** | Radioactive ruins of the once-bustling center |
| **Old Corporate Center** | Ruin of Night City's Corporate heart |
| **Old Medical Center** | Largely irradiated; hospitals buried under rubble |

## The Rebuilding Urban Center

**Threat Rating:** Corporate / Moderate

Dense urban core that survived the bomb. Seawall damage causes periodic flooding; power and water are unreliable. NCART subway runs when not flooded; monorail rebuild planned. Zhirafa mechs and private armies patrol rising Corp towers. **Watson Urban Reconstruction Zone** megabuildings house War refugees; **Kabuki** (Watson) holds much of Night City's Asian population.

## The Combat Zones

**Threat Rating:** Combat

Gangs rule slums, tenements, and ruins. No reconstruction; occasional Corp or City "kudzu trimming" massacres. See CP:R p. 310 for location list.

### Old Japantown

Once a Japanese cultural center; fell to chaos in the 4th Corporate War. Most population moved to Watson; gangs dominate empty blocks. Tyger Claws protect rebuilding efforts elsewhere.

| # | Location | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| 29 | **Crisis Medical Center** | Advanced no-questions care; neutral faction ground |
| 30 | **Highcourt Plaza Hotel** | Classy, well-defended; 1920s decor between Japantown and Little China |

## The Overpacked Suburbs

**Threat Rating:** Moderate / Combat

Central Night City lost utilities after the War; suburbs absorbed tent cities and unregulated refugee camps on former Corporate Beavervilles. Megabuildings under construction but region stays crowded and crime-ridden.

### Heywood (Santo Domingo)

Overpacked sector; rich north, poor south (locals call the south **Santo Domingo**).

| # | Location | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| 36 | **Aldecaldo Camp** | Edge-of-city Nomad trade hub; badlands guides and transport |
| 37 | **City Police Precinct #2** | Overworked; half the force on scavvers; Zhirafa drone patrols |
| 38 | **MetalStorm** | Indestructible chromer bar under the bridge to central NC |

### Heywood Industrial Zone

Largest industrial zone — warehouses, factories, derelict cargo ships.

| # | Location | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| 39 | **Zhirafa Office Park** | Built atop old factories; drones and animal mecha on patrol |

### New Westbrook

Built in ruins of the old Executive Zone; still glitzy but packed with War refugees and gentrification displaced.

| # | Location | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| 40 | **Network 54 Offices** | Neon 54 skyline marker; studio housing for stars |
| 41 | **Night City Firestation #1** | AV and truck deployment citywide |
| 42 | **Rocklin Augmentics Campus** | Offices, cafeteria, hospital; R&D tower via skybridges |
| 43 | **WorldSat Offices** | Ridge-top comms tower; heavily defended |

### Rancho Coronado

Vast old Beaverville tract housing overtaken by tent cities and industrial sprawl from dead mini-malls.

## The Open Road

**Threat Rating:** Outskirts

Highways once ruled by gogangers; Nomad Families increasingly secure routes with surplus military hardware.

**Highways:** CA-41, CA-46, CA-58, CA-99, CA-828, I-5, I-101
