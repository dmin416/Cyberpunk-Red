## Premise

Marshal is already slipping.

In the early stages of **cyberpsychosis**, he is running too hot, too detached, and too ready to read every threat as permission to escalate. While moving alone through **Kabuki**, he catches what looks like a Tyger Claw snatch crew forcing a woman off **Sakura Street** and into a stash flat above a pachinko parlor.

He was not hired for this. There is no fixer, no briefing, and no clean incentive beyond the fact that he sees it happening and decides he is not letting them take her.

**What Marshal sees:** Four Claws manhandling a struggling woman into a side door. One has a grip on her arm. She is not going quietly.

**What is actually happening (GM only):** The woman is **Aya Kurosawa**, daughter of a mid-tier Kimen-Gumi captain. The four men are her **sitters** — Hanmen assigned to keep her alive after a rival crew made a play earlier that night. They are not kidnapping her. They are extracting her to a Claw safe flat above the parlor, rough because she is drunk, furious, and trying to go back into the street where the rival might still be watching. From twenty meters away, through Marshal's spiraling lens, it reads as a snatch.

That choice turns the night into a personal bloodbath. Marshal goes in alone. Once the shooting starts, he has to decide whether he can still tell the difference between rescue and indulgence — and whether the "victim" is what he thought she was.
