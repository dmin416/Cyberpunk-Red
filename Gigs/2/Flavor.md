# Flavor — Gig 2

Setting, look, and dialogue beats for Cleric. Pair with [Layout.md](Layout.md) and [Enemies.md](Enemies.md).

---

## Where

**Old Combat Zone** — southeast fringe, a few blocks in from the Glen / Precinct #1 border watch.

Threat Rating: **Combat**. NCPD will brief you at the cordon and will not follow you past the tape. C-SWAT / Max-Tac is tied up elsewhere in the Zone (another chrome incident, or a gang flare — pick one). This is why the job is on the Street board.

**Why this building type lives here:** Pre-War cube hotels and three-story walk-up tenements. No reconstruction. Gangs strip what they want; the rest rot. An abandoned mid-rise with a wired psycho inside is exactly the kind of problem the City farms out to edgerunners.

*Nearby flavor (optional):* Jesse James' Kosher Deli deeper in; Generation Red's booby-trapped clubhouse is the same neighborhood energy if you want a local comparison.

---

## Appearance

**Exterior**

Three stories of stained ferrocrete and peeling Habitex cladding. Old cube-hotel signage half-ripped — letters left spell something like `…UBE` or a dead chain name nobody uses. Ground-floor windows boarded or barred; upper windows dark, a few cracked. Front door chained and then *re*-barricaded from the inside with scrap and furniture. Alley fire exit has a rusted push-bar and a wire you shouldn't trust. Smell: piss, ozone, old cooking oil, and something metallic from the traps.

Street is empty in a bad way — locals cleared out when the screaming started. NCPD tape and one marked cruiser at the corner. No AV overhead. No Trauma Team contract on this block.

**Interior**

Narrow halls, cube-doors every few meters, shared bath smells that never left. Power is intermittent — psycho runs what he needs off scavenged batteries and building leftovers. Walls scarred with tool marks, blood smears wiped wrong, tripwires at ankle and chest height. Floor 2 opened into a kill box: drywall torn out between units, sightlines down the hall, brass and grenade pins on the floor. Nest smells like sweat, gun oil, and unwashed chrome. Floor 3 is quieter — dust, a child's shoe, a closet door that doesn't quite close.

## NCPD (cordon briefing)

**Officer on scene:** Tired patrol, Precinct #1 border detail. Not Max-Tac. They want this off their board before shift change.

**Tone:** Professional, blunt, slightly ashamed they're outsourcing a psycho.

### Openers

- "You're the contractors. Good. Badge stays on this side of the tape. You go in, you own whatever happens."
- "C-SWAT's stacked on another call. We don't have a team for a three-story killbox tonight. City's paying Street rates — Hazardous. You in or not?"
- "Building's been dark three days. Neighbors heard chrome and screaming, then nothing. We pulled a perimeter. That's the job description."

### Briefing beats (give freely)

- **Target:** One cyberpsycho. Heavy chrome. EMP's gone — don't bother talking him down.
- **Pay:** City / NCPD — **2,500eb** on confirmed kill. Hazardous Street rates.
- **Objective:** Flatline the chrome. **Also** extract a survivor — kid, maybe eight, ten. Only the kid's still answering when someone shouts from the street (once, yesterday). No contact since.
- **Parents:** Behind the tape. They made it to the cordon. They're offering **500eb** out of pocket if you bring their child out alive. Not City money — theirs.
- **Building:** Old cube hotel / walk-up. He's had time. Expect traps. We already lost a drone in the lobby — feed died on a flash.
- **Rules of engagement:** Kill authorized. Try not to drop the building on the block. Kid comes out breathing or the parents' money doesn't move — and Legal gets ugly on the City side too.
- **Support:** None inside. Radio if you clear it; we'll send a wagon for the body. Trauma's on you unless you bought a card.

### Pushback / color

- "Why us?" → "Because you're cheaper than a Max-Tac stack and you're already standing here."
- "Backup?" → "You get us holding the street. That's backup."
- "Description of the psycho?" → "Male, full arms, subdermal, moves like he owns the stairwell. One witness said 'a big fucking pipe and a smile that wasn't a smile.' That's all we got."
- "The kid?" → "Name unknown. Small. Hiding. Parents are right over there — don't make us write 'collateral' on the sheet."
- "Parents paying?" → "Five hundred. Their money. We pay twenty-five hundred for the chrome. You want both, bring both results."

### On success / failure (radio)

- Clear (kill + kid): "Copy. Wagon's rolling. Parents are waiting. Nice work — don't stick around for the screamsheets."
- Kill only: "…Fuck. Bring what you can. City still owes you for the chrome. Parents' money dies with the kid."
- Kid only / psycho not confirmed: "Kid's out — good. Kill fee waits until that thing's cold."
- You dead: they don't answer. Someone else gets the board listing tomorrow.

## Parents (cordon)

**Tone:** Exhausted, raw, trying not to scream at cops who won't go in. Not edgerunners. They have **500eb** liquid and will hand it over the second they see their child breathing.

### Lines

- "Please. We'll pay. Five hundred — it's what we have. Just bring them out."
- "The police won't go past the tape. You're going in. Right? You're going in."
- "They answered yesterday. Once. We heard them. They're still in there."
- (on return, child alive) "Oh god— oh god— here. Take it. Take all of it."
- (on return, child dead / empty-handed) "…No. No, you said— you *said*—" (no pay; NCPD may pull them back from the line)

## Cyberpsycho lines

**Voice:** Flat, then sudden volume. Talks to the building, the traps, and "intruders" as the same problem. Not cartoon ranting — methodical, offended that anyone walks his geometry.

### Ambient (Crew not in sight — mutter / speakers / yelling down stairs)

- "Wire's tight. Plate's live. Good."
- "Footsteps. Wrong footsteps."
- "This is *mine*. The stairs are *mine*."
- "Come on. Come on. Test it."

### When a trap goes off (success for him)

- "See? See? You don't listen."
- "Loud. Good. I know where you are."
- "One less. Keep coming."

### When a trap is disarmed / bypassed

- "No. No, that's not— you don't get to *fix* it."
- "Cheating. You're cheating."
- "Fine. Fine. Closer, then."

### First visual / combat open

- "Max-Tac? …No. Cheap. Street meat."
- "Kid stays. Kid's quiet. You're not."
- "I built this. You don't leave."

### In the fight

- "Down the hall. I *own* the hall."
- "Armor's loud. Heart's louder."
- "Grenade. Smile. Again."
- "Pipe remembers better than I do."
- (if hurt) "Pain Editor says no. I say no."
- (if kid mentioned) "Mine. Found it. Kept it. You don't take nesting material."

### If the Crew goes for the child first

- "Wrong floor. Wrong. Come back to the wire."
- "Leave the quiet one. Fight the loud ones."
- (moving upstairs) "You touched my quiet. Now I touch yours."

### Downed / last breath (optional)

- "Stairs… still mine…"
- Or silence. Chrome twitching. No speech.

## Child lines

**Voice:** Small, hoarse from crying / dust. Short sentences. Freezes if shouted at. Stealth 8 — they only talk when they think it's safe.

### Heard through a door / from the closet (before trust)

- "…Is it the metal man?"
- "Please don't open it. Please."
- "I didn't touch the wires. I didn't."
- "Mommy said stay. I stayed."

### When the Crew identifies as rescue

- "Are you police?"
- "He said if I make noise the floor eats people."
- "I can be quiet. I'm good at quiet."
- "Don't step on the shiny string. It bites."

### On the move (MOVE 4 — scared, slow)

- "It's too fast. Wait—"
- "The stairs are bad. The stairs are *bad*."
- "Carry? …Okay. Okay."
- "Is he still downstairs?"

### If a trap pops nearby

- (scream, then) "I told you— I *told* you—"
- "Make it stop. Please make it stop."

### If they see the psycho

- Wordless. Or: "Metal man. Metal man—" and they try to hide behind whoever's closest.

### Outside / clear

- "Is it over?"
- "I want… I want water."
- "Don't leave me with the tape people." (NCPD — trust is thin)

## Conclusion (after the kill)

**Resolution:** Psycho down. Child to **parents** at the tape (or not). NCPD takes the corpse; Crew strips salvage before the wagon arrives.

**Cash:** **2,500eb** NCPD (confirmed kill) + **500eb** parents (child alive) = **3,000eb** full clear. See Premise reward table for partials.

**Loot rule:** Sheet chrome looks like ~10k on paper. After inspection, **salvageable weapons + cyberware = 3,000eb total** (Hazardous loot limit — separate from cash). The rest is scrap, fused, or too far gone for a ripperdoc to want.

### Narration — first glance

- At first glance it's a goldmine. Two chrome arms, popup hardware still warm, subdermal plating, a linear frame under the meat — Night Market money lying in a pool of its own coolant.
- The SMG, the pipe, the grenades on the belt: enough iron to make a Fixer smile.
- For a second the room smells like payday.

### Narration — the truth

- Then you get closer. The popup mounts are cracked from overuse — someone forced the cycles until the housings warped.
- Myomar in the arms is cooked; actuators seized mid-flex. This chrome wasn't maintained. It was *ridden*.
- The Sigma frame has stress fractures along the spine mounts. Jump boosters on the legs are blown — pistons bent, seals dry.
- Subdermal armor is ablated in ugly patches, not from your fight alone — months of traps, ricochets, and self-inflicted geometry.
- Neuralware's a write-off: Pain Editor socket melted, Kerenzikov fried to black glass. Whatever kept him standing wasn't going to survive a rip.
- Half the "wealth" is fused to bone and slag. A ripperdoc would charge you to haul it to the dump.

### Narration — what you actually get

- What comes free clean: the Very Heavy Melee (scuffed but solid), the Heavy SMG (needs a scrub, still shoots), a couple of live grenades, and enough intact limb/plating scrap a chop-doc will pay for.
- You bag it. You weigh it. You do the math.
- **Three thousand eurobucks.** Not the jackpot the corpse promised — just what the Street pays when a psycho burns through his own chrome before you ever pull the trigger.

### Optional beats

- **Kid watching:** "You're taking his arms?" / "He said the metal was hungry."
- **NCPD on radio:** "Wagon's two out. Whatever's still attached is evidence — whatever's in your bag is your problem."
- **Failed rescue:** Same salvage math; NCPD still pays **2,500eb** for the kill if chrome is confirmed dead; parents pay **nothing**.

### Suggested 3,000eb salvage split (GM fiat)

| Salvage | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Very Heavy Melee Weapon | 500eb |
| Heavy SMG (functional) | 100eb |
| Live grenades (AP / thrown leftovers) | ~400eb |
| Intact cyberlimb / plating scrap (sellable) | ~2,000eb |
| **Total** | **3,000eb** |

Burned / worthless: popup housings, Jump Boosters, Neural Link suite, most of the Sigma, Hardened Shielding, cooked Grafted Muscle.
