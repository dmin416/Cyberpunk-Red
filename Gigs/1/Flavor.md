# Flavor — Kabuki Kill Floor (Gig 1)

Bar slaughter in Kabuki. Pair with [Layout.md](Layout.md), [Enemies.md](Enemies.md), [Friends.md](Friends.md).

---

## Where

**Kabuki** — a Claw-friendly bar off the main neon (not the Afterlife; not fancy). Half-masks drink here. Kimen-Gumi money keeps the private room booked.

Threat: **Moderate** with Combat manners. NCPD is a punchline. Trauma shows if someone paid.

---

## Weather today

**Light rain** · **Hot** (~80°F / 27°C) · Normal

The sky weeps an oily drizzle, slicking the streets with a treacherous, rainbow-hued sheen that distorts neon reflections. The air grows thick with wet decay and hot metal. Jackets steam when someone hits the door. If the temperature drops, this turns to fine, sharp sleet that clicks against glass and makes every metal surface a freezing hazard to the touch.

**Gameplay:** **−1** to appropriate Skill checks — **Athletics** on slippery footing (fence, wet ferrocrete, bar stool), **Drive Land Vehicle** to stay on the road. Same penalty indoors where rain is tracked in on boots.

## Appearance

**Interior:** Low light, red paper lanterns, sticky floor, pachinko bleed through the wall. Oni masks that aren't décor. Private room door in back — nicer wood, worse people. Feels like a Kabuki **izakaya** that never got a Yum Seng seafood budget.

**Street:** Oily light rain on wet ferrocrete — rainbow puddles swallowing neon. **2–3 CHOOH² Roadbikes** at the curb (Claws are known for fast bikes — CP:R). Half-mask stickers on tanks; one tiger-stripe seat cover. A dented **Compact Groundcar**. Rain beads on chrome. When the fight spills, Kabuki watches from doorways and pretends not to.

## O (narrator)

**Tupper prefix:** `narratoro:`

**O** runs table narration in Discord — **third person**, present tense, sensory and concrete. O does not speak in-scene; NPCs do. O **never** addresses players or NPCs as **you**. Name PCs when needed (Marshal, Weaver); otherwise describe actions, space, and sensation from outside any single head. After a player posts, O acknowledges what they did, then advances the beat (new detail, consequence, or pressure). No exposition dumps; stakes sit in the texture.

**Observation rule:** Only describe what PCs can see, hear, or smell from where they are. Do not front-load interior from the street, alley depth from the sidewalk, or “money behind the door” until they cross the threshold or clock the back room. Drop beats when they look, move, or enter.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Smell, sound, touch, space (cramped / slick / loud) | Read stat blocks or DVs aloud unless asked |
| Night City + book terms (Kibble, SCOP, Smash, CHOOH²) | Purple prose or lore lectures |
| Short paragraphs; one beat per post | Steal player choices |
| Weather, crowd, door, private room as pressure | Spoil unobserved rooms or infer hidden wealth from the facade |
| Gate detail by position (street → door → inside → back → alley) | Dump the whole location in one post |
| Third person; `narratoro:` prefix | **You**, **your**, or speaking *to* anyone in the narration |
| Direct prose; no hedging ("suggestion of") | Vague observation filler |
| No comma before **and** or **but** | `..., and` / `..., but` (use a period or rephrase) |
| No em dashes | `—` |

### Exterior — approach / street

*Use if PCs are outside or narrating arrival. Nothing from inside the bar.*

```
narratoro: Kabuki sits off the main strip, away from the fancier neon of Afterlife territory, wedged between a shuttered noodle stall and a taller tenement block whose lower windows are boarded over. The building is low and squat, a single story of patched concrete, a sagging awning stretched out front. The sign flickers between kanji and a cracked English translation, half the tubes dead.

The tenement beside it rises maybe six stories, laundry lines strung between rusted balconies, most of the lights dark this late. Across the street a row of shopfronts sit behind roll down security gates, graffiti and old flyers layered over the metal. Cracked ferrocrete sidewalk runs the length of the block, potholes filled with standing rainwater.
```

*Optional — curb in view:* two or three CHOOH² roadbikes, half-mask stickers on the tanks, one tiger-stripe seat cover. A dented compact groundcar. Rain on chrome.

### Alley — mouth (from main street)

*Walking the block; alley visible from the sidewalk, not entered.*

```
narratoro: An alley cuts along Kabuki's side wall between the bar and the tenement, a narrow slot walled in concrete and brick. Dumpsters line one wall, overflowing, mostly lost in shadow. A fire escape bolts to the tenement above. At the far end, a flicker of light where the alley opens onto a side street. Garbage wafts on the wet air. Rain drips from the awning edge and from the fire escapes overhead.
Muffled noise leaks out from the bar, laughter and raised voices through the open door. Glass clinks against glass. Someone barks something in Japanese, followed by more laughing. Pachinko rattles faintly through the wall next door.
```

### Alley — mouth (look only)

*Peering in from the street or service door; still not entered.*

The alley runs between Kabuki and the tenement, concrete and brick, barely wide enough for two abreast. Dumpsters somewhere in the dark; overflow smelled before seen. A fire escape climbs the tenement wall. The far end opens to a side street; one streetlamp flickers at the exit. Puddles in the low points, oil-slick where light reaches.

### Alley — inside

*Entered the alley.*

Same narrow run: dumpsters along one wall, crates stacked under the fire escape, wet brick, hot rain dripping from the awning edge above. Footing slick (**−1 Athletics** if it matters). The side street at the far end, or back toward the bar's service door.

### Interior — threshold / main room

*Front door opens.*

The door cuts the rain off. Warm air rolls out first: synth-sake, grilled SCOP, cheap incense, wet ferrocrete and CHOOH² fumes trailing whoever just came in. Pachinko rattles through the wall from the parlor next door, electronic static under everything.

Red paper lanterns along the ceiling beams; dim red light over the room. Sticky floor, worse near the door. Vinyl booths on one wall, cracked and taped; low tables with half-finished drinks and bowls of edamame. A scarred bar rail on the other side, wobbly wood stools bolted down at odd intervals. Oni masks on the walls between the lanterns, old wood and lacquer, teeth bared.

*At the rail (if scene opens inside):*

Marshal and Weaver at the rail, drinks in hand. Across the room, half-masks at a booth: Gang Colors over armorjack, jackets open in the heat. A *tokkuri* and ochoko between them. Beer cans. A plastic cup of Smash. One of them laughs too loud. A rifle leans against the booth like furniture. Masks up on foreheads while they drink.

*Player beat.*

### Interior — back of house (observed)

*Someone looks toward the rear, moves back, or clocks the private door.*

Past the booths and open floor, a back hall runs toward the toilets and a service door, kegs stacked along the wall, tile slick from tracked-in rain. Set apart from the rest of the room, a private door in nicer wood than anything else in the building. Muffled sound if any; the fit of the frame.

## Claw lines

Mix English streetslang with Japanese. Gloss in parentheses for the GM — don't read the gloss aloud.

### Wave 1 (floor)

- "The fuck you looking at, chrome?"
- 「何見てんだ、ハク？」 *(Nani miten da, haku? — What're you staring at, chrome?)*

- 「座れ。壊すぞ。」 *(Suware. Kowasu zo. — Sit. Or we break you.)*

- 「裏だ！裏の部屋！」 *(Ura da! Ura no heya! — Back! The back room!)*

## Breach — after combat

**Mandatory.** Runs after all combat ends. See [Friends.md](Friends.md#breach--after-combat-mandatory).

### The promise (inverted)

At Roxy's, Breach handed Marshal the **AR+GL** so Marshal could kill him if he hurt innocents. Marshal handed Breach the **etched X-9mm** and called them friends.

Tonight Breach doesn't ask who won the bar. He asks: **did Marshal go psycho first?** Is anyone still home inside the chrome that painted Kabuki red?

### O — arrival

> Gunfire's done. Rain on hot metal. You're standing in what used to be a booth — or on the slick street under neon — and the Tyger Claws are bodies or runners. Doesn't matter.
>
> Someone's watching. Not helping. Not shooting Claws. A green hoodie in the wet dark. You know the walk before you see the face.
>
> He doesn't look at Weaver. Doesn't look at the half-masks. Looks at **you**.
>
> "Marshal."
