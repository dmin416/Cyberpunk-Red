Kabuki sits off the main strip, away from the fancier neon of Afterlife territory, wedged between a shuttered noodle stall and a taller tenement block whose lower windows are boarded over. The building itself is low and squat, a single story of patched concrete, a sagging awning stretched out front. Cracked ferrocrete sidewalk runs the length of the block, potholes filled with standing rainwater.
