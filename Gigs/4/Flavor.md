Street slaughter by a sushi stall. Same Claw energy as [Gig 1](../1/Flavor.md), no bar. Pair with [Layout.md](Layout.md), [Enemies.md](Enemies.md).

## Where

**Kabuki**: a narrow side street off the main neon, one block from the pachinko bleed. A **sushi stall** (synth fish, real rice, cheap wasabi) does steady night trade. Tyger Claws treat the curb like a toll booth they never charge for. They just own the block.

Threat: **Moderate** with Combat manners. NCPD is a punchline. Trauma shows if someone paid.

---

## Appearance

**Stall:** Steam over the counter, blue plastic stools, paper lantern with a carp on it. Chef's knife flash, octopus tentacle synth sign in the window. Smells like vinegar, soy and hot oil.

**Street:** Wet ferrocrete, neon in the puddles. **Semi active traffic**: compact groundcars, delivery drones, a honking line that never quite stops. **2 to 3 CHOOH² Roadbikes** at the curb (tiger stripe seat, half mask sticker on one tank). When guns come out, Kabuki watches from shutters and pretends not to.

**Exec 4** looks wrong here on purpose. Good shoes, corporate cut, eating with the crew anyway.

### Post 1: Watson and the market strip

You are in the **Rebuilding Urban Center**, on the edge of the **Watson Development**. CP:R calls this a district still going up: megabuilding ribs and formocrete modules, crane arms, construction arc light bleeding into the block. Much of Night City's Asian population lives in the Watson neighborhood people call **Kabuki**.

The market strip is open late. Booth canvas, plastic stools, cred scanners on rope counters. A **Data Term** sits on the corner in its armored concrete post. Thirty percent of Night City streets still have one. This one flickers a map of the ward and a news crawl from a local screamsheet publisher.

Across the lane, shop shutters are down. Graffiti over old flyers. The sidewalk is patched ferrocrete from the post War years. Twisted asphalt still shows in places where the fill shifted. NCTC buses move the major thoroughfare when the route is not flooded. Tonight the lane is live. Compact groundcars. A delivery drone. Honking.

Book note on the city at large: on crowded streets it is not uncommon to see as many guns as briefcases. Kabuki is **Moderate** threat with Combat manners. Lawmen are a punchline until someone paid for trauma.

### Post 2: the stall and the curb

The eat spot is a **Kabuki Market** counter, not a bar. Steam from a propane ring. Synth salmon on rice. Real wasabi is a markup. Most street meals here sit between **Prepack** pricing and a **Vendit** pull: CP:R lists **sushi** and **ramen** in the food column of a standard Vendit table. This stall is the hands on version. A cook working a knife over a plank. Miso in a bowl. Tea in paper cups.

A **Vendit** hums ten meters down. Thick steel housing. CP:R warns some carry an automated flamethrower turret. Nobody is swiping it tonight.

**Your curb:** stools, plates half finished, kit within reach. Weaver's gear is close. Exec's corporate cut reads wrong against **Asia Pop** and **Gang Colors** on the passing crowd. Solo has the sightlines. Medtech's bag is hooked under the bench.

**Three CHOOH² roadbikes** idle at the angle Tyger Claws prefer. CP:R lists the Roadbike as a common CHOOH² powered bike. The gang entry in *Welcome to Night City* names them for **fast bikes**, **enhanced reflexes** and **killer martial arts**. Light armored biker jackets. Half masks up while they eat. *Screamsheets* turf war text puts Tyger Claws in **light armored biker jackets** with blades and heavy pistols. These three have SMGs and knives in the open.

Traffic does not stop for dinner. Headlights cut the lane. The city keeps moving.
