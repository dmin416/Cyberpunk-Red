## Premise

**Rio Mendez** runs **Last Stop**, a food truck bolted to a permanent bay in **Stack Nine** — cargo-container housing on the **Rancho Coronado** fringe where asphalt dies and the Badlands begin.

Her morning regular (and current crush), Rockerboy **Juno Vale**, skipped Koff Pop **two mornings** running. She hired local PI **Marcus Hale** out of the **Crosscut** cube hotel. Hale hasn’t checked in either.

Rio can’t float Hazardous cash. **Reef** (Stack Nine lot mechanic / Nomad Fixer) — and through him Nomad Fixer **Woodchipper**, who uses Hale — covers the **street payout** if the Crew brings people back, or proves they burned daylight trying. Rio still puts up **meal vouchers** and gratitude.

**There is no shootout waiting.** Opposition is **time**, sun, dust, rubble, and two people bleeding out under a beige Metrocar. Free and stabilize them **before morning** or both die. Timer + split Tech/Medtech is what keeps this **Hazardous**.

### What actually happened (GM only)

Juno camped ~1 mile north for “isolation inspiration,” Agent left behind so the city couldn’t ping them. Mid-night **Raffen** gunfire spooked them into biking home blind. They hit an outcrop, wrecked the bike, broke a leg — stranded. Hale traced the campsite via Juno’s Agent, drove out in his **Zonda Metrocar**, caught a **dust storm**, rammed a ridge, and brought the rockslide down on both of them. They’ve been broiling under rubble since.

The Crew doesn’t get that speech. They get empty stools, a cold coffee cup, and a clock.

## The Clock (GM spine)

Session opens **morning** (~0800–0900) at Last Stop. Victims die at **next dawn** (~0600) if not stabilized. Signal the day in fiction, not a digital countdown.

| Phase | Rough time | Fiction pressure |
|-------|------------|------------------|
| **Morning** | Hook → noon | Line at the truck; Rio’s voice cracks once |
| **Heat** | Noon → 1600 | Endurance on walks; tracks harder; “sun’s cooking that ridge” |
| **Amber** | 1600 → dusk | Locals ask if you found them; Reef texts once if they have his number |
| **Night** | Dusk → 0200 | Headlamps; cold; optional scav / distant Raffen flavor |
| **Last hours** | 0200 → dawn | Vitals fiction worsens when they open the car; no more leisurely plans |

**Burn rules of thumb (adjust to table pace):**

| Choice | Typical cost |
|--------|--------------|
| Full Stack Nine canvass | 30–60 min |
| Crosscut (all three sites, no flailing) | 45–90 min |
| Walk campsite | <1 hour + Endurance |
| Drive campsite | 15–20 min |
| Spiral search (Tactics success) | ~30 min |
| Wander search | ~+1 hour |
| Wait for Nomad tow / Kel’s GRAF3 | 30–90 min (relationship + luck) |
| Overnight camp at ridge | Burns to morning — **both die** unless already stabilized |

If the table stalls, advance a phase and show it (shadows, Rio Agent ping, wind).

- **Tech** — Elec/Sec (shutters, cubes, Agent), Basic Tech (levers), Drive (tow), Demolitions (last resort)

## CLIMAX — Search & Rescue

### Nav to campsite

With Hale’s map notes / Agent pin: **DV13** Library Search, Local Expert (Badlands), or Wilderness Survival.  
Walk = **under 1 hour**. Walking under sun: **DV13 Endurance** or **−2** all Checks until a full night’s sleep.  
Vehicle = faster; still need track/spiral from the tree.

Campsite is broken down — cold fire ring only. Dust ate bike/car tracks.

| Method | DV / time |
|--------|-----------|
| Tracking | **DV15** |
| Perception (tracks) | **DV17** |
| Tactics → spiral search | **DV15** → find car in **~30 min** |
| Random wandering | Find car in **~1 more hour** |

### The wreck

Hale’s Metrocar is **pinned under landslide rubble** at a ridge base. Twisted bike scrap nearby. **DV15 Perception** → signs of life inside. Rubble blocks casual reach.

**Freeing them** (player plans; sample DVs):

| Plan | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Hand-move rubble | Needs **2+** people with **BODY 10+** working together; no Check |
| Tow with vehicle | **DV17 Drive** if Heavy Chassis; **DV24** without. Fail = rubble still moves, **vehicle takes 3d6** damage (or 1 Serious step) |
| Explosives | ≥2 grenades (or equiv.) + **DV24 Demolitions**. **Fail = both NPCs die** |
| Levers / fulcrums | Right gear + **DV21 Basic Tech** |
| Call help | **Reef / Woodchipper** Nomad assist, or **Kel Sato** (local builder, **GRAF3**). Social **DV13–17** by relationship + urgency. Help costs **clock**, not cash. |
| Partial shift (optional) | GM may allow one body reachable first on a partial success — then Medtech triages while Tech finishes the car. Split the party’s attention on purpose. |

### Medical (once free)

Both at **0 HP**, **not stabilized**. Don’t move them until stabilized (CP:R healing rules). Detail sheets: [Enemies.md](Enemies.md).

| Victim | Critical Injuries |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Marcus Hale** | Broken Ribs · Cracked Skull · Whiplash |
| **Juno Vale** | Broken Leg · Foreign Object · Spinal Injury · Whiplash |

**Field order that feels right:** Stabilize HP → don’t yank Spinal/Cracked Skull around → load for cheap clinic (Rio knows a stack-side medbay / “pay what you can” doc) → celebration later.

Suggested Checks (tune to table):

| Action | Sample DV |
|--------|-----------|
| Stabilize one victim (First Aid / Paramedic) | **15** (dust, bad light, wreck angle) |
| Address Foreign Object enough to move | **17** Paramedic / Medicine |

→ Any death → **Memorial**

## Resolutions

### Memorial
Neighborhood vigil on spool tables. Reef still cuts the **partial Hazardous** check. Rio honors **7 vouchers each** but goes cold. Locals don’t blame out loud; eyes do.

### Celebration
Days later: thank-you at Last Stop after a cheap clinic stay. Reef pays **full clear**. Rio bumps to **10 vouchers each** + **free Koff Pop mornings** until the truck dies. Juno is a recruitable musician contact; Hale owes a favor (one free PI dig later).

### Seeds (optional, not mandatory)
- Concert chip → Media/Rocker intro
- Woodchipper remembers the Crew as “people who don’t leave Nomad PIs under rocks”
- Stack Nine treats the Crew as neighbors if both lived
