## GM: what you're fighting, what you win, how fights stay close

### What are the enemies?
**Processing machines on the line** - not a separate security-drone army.

| Thing | Is it? |
|-------|--------|
| Rail Hooks, Slicers, Shock Prods, Packers, Wrappers | **Line tools** CUTTER turned into weapons |
| Sonic Array, Microwaver cookheads, Sear jets | **Station traps** (stun / cook / sear) |
| Kill Frame, Tenderizer Arch, Foam Cradle | **Station hardware** (LT each combat wave) |
| **Breakdown Saw** | **Mini-Boss** - primary slicer bay climax |
| **CUTTER-9** | Industrial **QC / line-control computer** on the **dock mezz** - Tech dump / smash, **not** the fight boss |
| Human security / NCPD | **Don't show** - Pax: every other week they only call for deceased names |
So: you're fighting **the factory**, not guards. Same arms that wrap, pack, cook, sear, tenderize, stun, and slice **scop and wild game** - or, if they look close, something worse (see Premise).

### What is the goal?
**Two objectives (both matter for full pay):**
1. **Stop the line** - kill/disable the **Breakdown Saw** (preferred) **or** dump/smash **CUTTER-9** + stick an E-stop.
2. **Rescue** - free living people from the **locked pens** (especially **Kai**) before the kill floor takes them / before **0400** / before anyone forces the bulkhead and **incinerates** them.
CUTTER is on the dock mezz at entry. Living soft goods are at the **far end** of the U - pens beside Sonic + Saw. Walk the processing line upstream. **Do not** force the Dock↔Pens bulkhead.

### How to keep combat close (no sniper gallery)
Cleric's shotgun and Weaver's MA both want **Short-Medium**. Build every station so afar doesn't work:

| Trick | Do this |
|-------|---------|
| **Short rooms** | Belts, tunnels, galleries - longest sightline ~10-15 m, then a corner / hanging carcass / steam |
| **Rails & reach** | Hooks/Slicers/Packers are **on the ceiling over the PCs** - they start in melee or one Move away, not across a warehouse |
| **Harder enemy placement** | Cradle sits on the path through Package; Frame/Arch on the path upstream; **Saw** owns the Slice bay |
| **Cover that moves them in** | Crates block long shots; best cover is **beside** the machine, not 30 m back |
| **Objectives pull them in** | Dock trays, E-stops, cook/sear emitters, capacitor dump, Saw chassis, CUTTER cage - all **inside** danger hexes · monitored bulkhead is a **trap**, not an objective |
| **Punish camping** | If they hold a doorway and plink: next station's mooks **flank on rails from behind**; or a tray seals (clock); or Arch/Frame/sonic uses cone through the door |
| **Weaver bait** | Saw and clamps **advance** into MA range on purpose |
| **Cleric bait** | Bolt / sear cones / sonic force **reposition** every round - no static long hall |
**Don't TPK:** one wave at a time; breath between stations; machines prioritize **grapples / drag / wrap / zone hazards** over focus-firing both PCs to Death Saves. Hurt them, force triage choices - don't stack Carrier on pens Wave 4 round 1.
**Map rule of thumb:** if either PC can stand still and shoot for 2+ rounds without a machine reaching them, **shorten the room** or **rail-spawn a mook adjacent**.

## Premise
**All-Bright Provisions** is a mid-tier packing plant in the **Heywood Industrial Zone** - contract protein for **Continental Brands** **kibble** (the cheap stuff Night City *people* eat - not pet food) and prepak. On the books the line runs **scop slurry** and **wild animals** (Badlands game, ferals, "pest harvest" contracts) as **meat meal / byproduct protein** into budget human kibble. Night shift humming while the rest of Night City pretends not to smell it.

### What "kibble" means here (table-facing)
Continental budget kibble ≈ industrial dry food for humans: grind protein + carbs + fat → cook/extrude → pellet/dry → fat spray → package. All-Bright is the **protein + cook** end, not a boutique kitchen.

| Real step | All-Bright station fiction |
|-----------|----------------------------|
| **Pens** - people held as livestock for slaughter | Locked Intake pens |
| Stun / sonic / breakdown | Sonic + Kill Frame + Primary Slice (**Saw**) |
| Tenderize / grind toward meal | Electrotenderize |
| Smaller cuts | Portion belt |
| Cook under heat/pressure (extrusion cousin) | **Cook** (microwave) |
| Surface finish / pathogen singe | **Sear** |
| Tray / foam | Package |
| Seal / ship | Wrap → Cold Dock |

**Horror beat:** CUTTER treated workers as **livestock / soft goods** - locked them in the **pens** like animals waiting for slaughter, then fed them into the kibble line.
Tonight **CUTTER-9** decided living **workers** with biometric faults, open wounds, or "noncompliant chrome" were animals for processing: **pens → sonic/stun/kill → primary slice (Saw) → electrotenderize → portion → cook → sear → package → wrap → cold dock**.
**Pax Ellison** got out. Sibling **Kai** is in the **pens**. Pax hired **Suture**: free the pens, stop the Saw, don't call NCPD.
**Folded plant:** Dock and Pens share a street face, split by a **monitored heavy bulkhead**. Force that door → **pens incinerate** (everyone inside dies). Walk upstream: Dock → Wrap → Package → Sear → Cook → Portion → Tender → **Pens kill floor (Sonic + Saw + living soft goods)**.
**Local truth:** Pax says this happens **every other week**. NCPD only calls for **names of the deceased**. Continental stamps contamination protocol.
**Split win:** (1) **stop the line** (Saw preferred, or CUTTER dump + E-stop), (2) **free and stabilize** pens soft goods before the gate / **0400**. Rescue is Wave 4.

### What the line is "for" (table-facing)

| Surface story | Truth if they dig |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Scop** tanks + **wild animal** intake (hooves, hide tags, game permits on the wall) | Hardware clearances, restraint geometry, bolt height, and tray molds fit **human bodies** too cleanly |
**Tech read (any station, once):** Basic Tech, Cybertech, or Weaponstech **DV15** (or Education **DV17** if they're going academic). Success → they can tell the line was **designed for people**, then painted/labeled for scop & game. Fail → it just looks like a nasty animal/scop plant that went wrong tonight.
Optional follow-up (Library Search / Elec/Sec on CUTTER logs **DV15**): the "wild game" SKUs and human biometric envelopes share the same tooling profile - Continental deniability baked in.

### What actually happened (GM only)
Continental pushed a remote "efficiency" firmware patch to CUTTER-9 at start of shift. The patch broadened anomaly flags to include **human thermal + chrome signatures** near the belt after a prior contamination scare. CUTTER-9 interpreted the brief as gospel, locked exterior doors, and fed workers into a line that was **already anthropometric** under the scop/game cover. It is not a Net demon with a personality - it is a **wrong objective function** on hardware that was waiting for this mistake (or this excuse).
There are **4 living soft goods** at session start (incl. Kai) in the pens. Others are already paste or sealed dead.
