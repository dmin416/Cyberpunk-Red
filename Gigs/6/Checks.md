## Ceiling rails (GM ladder)
**GM only:** Combat machines ride the smaller outer rails. The central rail is for the Heavy Carrier later. Never say that out loud. Never stress that it is empty, waiting, or important.

| When | Check | What they learn *(facts only)* |
|------|-------|--------------------------------|
| First station (casual look up) | Perception **DV9** | There are three rails on the ceiling. The middle one is thicker. |
| After seeing 2+ enemy types move | Perception **DV13** | The moving arms hang on the two narrower outer rails. The middle rail is thicker. |
| Focused look / between waves | Perception **DV15** *or* Basic Tech **DV13** | Middle rail is heavier gauge. Hangers spaced wider. Fresh grease on the track. |
| Compare hanger spacing to animal tags / schematics they found | Deduction **DV15** *or* Education **DV15** | The middle hangers are sized for larger loads than the outer tool arms. |
| Jack a rail controller / wall schematic | Elec/Sec or Cybertech **DV15** | Tag: `MAINLINE_CARRIER` · status **IDLE / ARMED**. |
| *(GM only)* No one looked until Slice | - | Heavy Carrier can drop on Wave 4 pens bay without a prior hint. |
**Read-aloud (no check - they glance up):**
> Ceiling tracks carry arms above the belt. A thicker rail runs down the middle between them.
**Read-aloud (DV13 success - they watched the arms):**
> The moving arms hang from the two outer tracks. The middle rail is thicker than either of them.
**Read-aloud (DV15 / Tech DV13 - they measure):**
> The middle rail is heavier gauge. The hangers sit farther apart. The track looks freshly greased.
**GM use:** If they look up, describe hardware once. Do not repeat emptiness every station. Do not say nothing has used it, that it is waiting, or that something bigger is coming.
